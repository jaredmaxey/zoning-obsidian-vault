# Underwriting Index

## Overview

This folder contains the core analytical frameworks and concepts used in CRE underwriting — the process of projecting income, estimating value, sizing risk, and evaluating return adequacy for any commercial real estate investment or development. Notes are organized across four thematic groups: (1) pro forma building and data validation; (2) valuation methods; (3) risk and scenario analysis; and (4) development feasibility concepts.

## Contents

### Pro Forma Building and Data Validation

- [[Pro Forma Construction Method]] — Step-by-step method for building a CRE pro forma from gross potential rent to net operating income and projected returns.
- [[Rent Roll Analysis]] — Systematic review of a property's rent roll to assess in-place income quality, lease structure, rollover risk, and mark-to-market upside or downside.
- [[Expense Normalization]] — The process of adjusting a property's reported operating expenses to reflect sustainable, recurring costs, removing non-recurring items and correcting for owner idiosyncrasies.
- [[T-12 and T-3 Analysis]] — Review of a property's trailing 12-month and trailing 3-month operating statements to assess income stability, trend, and credibility of historical cash flows.
- [[Assumption Hierarchy: Actual vs. Pro Forma]] — The principle governing when to rely on actual operating history versus pro forma projections in CRE underwriting, with a defined hierarchy of evidence credibility.

### Valuation Methods

- [[Direct Capitalization]] — Single-period valuation method that divides stabilized NOI by a market-derived cap rate to estimate a property's current market value.
- [[Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)]] — Multi-period valuation and return analysis method that discounts projected property cash flows and terminal value to their present value, producing NPV and IRR.
- [[Residual Land Value Method]] — Development feasibility method that back-solves for the maximum land price a developer can pay while still achieving a target return, given projected revenues, costs, and yield requirements.

### Risk and Scenario Analysis

- [[Sensitivity Analysis]] — Framework for systematically varying key underwriting assumptions to quantify how changes in inputs affect deal returns and identify which variables most drive outcomes.
- [[Stress Testing]] — Application of defined adverse scenarios to a CRE pro forma to test whether a deal survives downturns in income, occupancy, or capital markets without breaching covenants or losing capital.
- [[Monte Carlo in CRE]] — Probabilistic simulation method that runs thousands of pro forma iterations with randomized inputs to produce a return distribution and quantify the probability of achieving target outcomes.

### Development Feasibility Concepts

- [[Yield-on-Cost vs. Market Cap Spread]] — The spread between a development's yield on cost and the prevailing market cap rate for comparable stabilized assets, used to measure development return adequacy and feasibility.
- [[Development Spread]] — The basis-point difference between a development's yield on cost and the market exit cap rate, quantifying the value creation premium that justifies development over acquisition.
