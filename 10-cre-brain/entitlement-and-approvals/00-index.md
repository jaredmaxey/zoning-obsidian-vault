# Entitlement and Approvals Index

## Overview

This folder contains 11 general, educational process notes covering the major steps in the U.S. land use entitlement and approval lifecycle. Notes are jurisdiction-general (`jurisdiction: general`) and describe how each process works across U.S. jurisdictions, with light Arizona/Phoenix-metro flavor where useful. Jurisdiction-specific versions of these notes (city-level zoning procedures, local fee schedules, specific statutory citations) will be built under `20-jurisdictions/` in later prompts.

Notes are ordered below by typical sequence in a development project — from the broadest policy-level approvals (general plan, rezoning) through project-specific approvals (PUD, CUP, variance, site plan, subdivision) and the cross-cutting processes that run in parallel (environmental review, public hearings, community outreach) to the final backstop (appeals).

## Contents

- [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] — Legislative process to amend a jurisdiction's long-range land use plan, typically required before a rezoning that conflicts with current plan designations.
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] — Legislative or quasi-legislative process to change a parcel's base zoning district, enabling uses or densities not permitted under the existing designation.
- [[Planned Unit Development (PUD)]] — Flexible entitlement mechanism allowing a project to depart from standard zoning regulations in exchange for a negotiated, project-specific development plan approved by the jurisdiction.
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] — Quasi-judicial approval allowing a conditionally permitted use to operate in a zoning district, subject to findings and project-specific conditions imposed by the decision-maker.
- [[Variance]] — Quasi-judicial relief from a specific development standard when strict application would cause undue hardship due to unique physical characteristics of the property.
- [[Site Plan Review]] — Administrative or quasi-judicial review of a project's detailed site layout for compliance with development standards, design guidelines, and approved entitlements before building permits are issued.
- [[Subdivision Approval]] — Process to legally divide or reconfigure land into lots, parcels, or condominium units, including infrastructure dedication and improvement requirements, governed by state subdivision law.
- [[Environmental Review Overview]] — Overview of federal and state environmental review requirements (NEPA, CEQA, and state equivalents) that apply to development projects and can significantly affect entitlement timelines and costs.
- [[Public Hearing Process]] — Procedural framework for public hearings in land use entitlement, including notice requirements, hearing conduct, the record, ex parte rules, and how hearing quality affects legal defensibility.
- [[Neighborhood Outreach]] — Strategic pre-application and pre-hearing community engagement process to identify concerns, build support, and reduce opposition risk for development projects requiring discretionary approval.
- [[Appeals Process]] — Administrative and judicial mechanisms for challenging or overturning land use entitlement decisions, including appeal timelines, standing, standard of review, and strategic considerations.
