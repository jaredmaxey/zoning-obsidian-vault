# Construction and Delivery Index

## Overview

This folder contains concept notes covering the full lifecycle of physical project delivery—from how costs are categorized and budgeted through how contracts are structured, how projects are scheduled and completed, and how construction risk is managed through insurance and bonding. These concepts are foundational to development underwriting, construction loan management, and ground-up development execution.

## Contents

### Cost Categories

- [[Hard Costs]] — Direct physical construction expenditures—site work, structure, MEP, and finishes—that become part of the permanent improvement.
- [[Soft Costs]] — Non-physical development expenditures including design fees, permits, financing costs, and carrying charges that support but do not become part of the physical building.
- [[FF&E]] — Furniture, fixtures, and equipment—movable personal property items budgeted and depreciated separately from the real property shell in hospitality, multifamily, and commercial development.
- [[General Conditions]] — The general contractor's direct project overhead—superintendent, field office, temporary utilities, and site logistics—billed separately from trade work and GC fee.
- [[Contingency: Hard vs. Soft]] — Separate reserve line items buffering hard cost overruns (design gaps, unforeseen conditions) and soft cost overruns (carry, fees, market shifts) in a development budget.

### Delivery Methods & Contracts

- [[GMP vs. Cost-Plus vs. Lump-Sum]] — The three principal construction contract pricing structures that determine how cost risk is allocated between owner and general contractor, each with distinct incentives and tradeoffs.
- [[Design-Build vs. Design-Bid-Build]] — Two fundamental project delivery methods distinguishing whether a single entity holds both design and construction responsibility or whether they are contracted separately and sequentially.
- [[Value Engineering]] — A systematic cost-reduction process that seeks to maintain functional performance while substituting lower-cost materials, systems, or construction methods during design or procurement.

### Schedule & Completion

- [[Construction Schedule and Critical Path]] — The timeline governing a construction project and the sequence of interdependent activities whose combined duration determines the earliest possible completion date.
- [[Certificate of Occupancy (CO)]] — The official government document certifying a building meets code requirements and may be legally occupied, marking the transition from construction to revenue-generating operations.
- [[Punchlist and Final Completion]] — The close-out process by which outstanding construction deficiencies are identified, corrected, and formally accepted, culminating in final payment release and retainage disbursement.

### Risk, Bonding & Insurance

- [[Builder's Risk Insurance]] — Property insurance covering a building under construction against physical loss or damage from fire, weather, theft, and other named perils during the construction period.
- [[Payment and Performance Bonds]] — Surety instruments guaranteeing that a contractor will complete the work (performance bond) and pay all subcontractors and suppliers (payment bond), protecting the owner and lien claimants.
