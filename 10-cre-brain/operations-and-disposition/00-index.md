# Operations and Disposition Index

## Overview

This folder covers the full lifecycle of CRE asset stewardship: from the day-to-day operational execution of property and asset management through the strategic capital decisions (refinance or sell), and finally the disposition mechanics (broker selection, tax planning, exit pricing). Notes are organized into three groups below: day-to-day operations, capital and hold-period decisions, and disposition and exit execution.

## Contents

### Operations

- [[Property Management Fundamentals]] — Core operational discipline that maintains physical assets, manages tenant relationships, collects rent, and controls expenses to protect and grow NOI.
- [[Asset Management vs. Property Management]] — Asset management sets investment strategy and directs capital decisions; property management executes day-to-day operations — two distinct but interdependent roles in a CRE ownership structure.
- [[Lease Administration]] — Systematic tracking and enforcement of all lease obligations, critical dates, rent escalations, and tenant rights across a property's tenancy roster.
- [[Rent Roll Maintenance]] — Live, unit-by-unit occupancy and rent record that is the primary data source for underwriting, lender reporting, and disposition due diligence on any income-producing property.
- [[CapEx Planning]] — The multi-year budgeting and prioritization of capital expenditures—both value-add improvements and deferred-maintenance remediation—essential to preserving asset value and executing the business plan.

### Capital Decisions

- [[Refinance Decision Framework]] — Structured process for deciding whether and when to refinance an existing loan, sizing the new proceeds, and evaluating the trade-off between cost of capital, hold period extension, and investor distributions.
- [[Hold vs. Sell Analysis]] — The quantitative and qualitative framework for deciding whether to continue holding an asset, refinance to recapitalize, or sell — the central disposition timing decision in asset management.

### Disposition and Exit

- [[Broker Selection and BOV]] — The process of selecting a listing broker and soliciting Broker Opinion of Value to establish market pricing expectations and execution strategy before launching a disposition.
- [[1031 Exchange Overview]] — IRC Section 1031 allows investors to defer capital gains and depreciation recapture taxes on a real property sale by reinvesting proceeds into a like-kind replacement property within statutory deadlines.
- [[Exit Cap Rate Derivation]] — The process of estimating the cap rate at which a property will sell at the end of the projected hold period, a critical assumption that drives terminal value in DCF models.
