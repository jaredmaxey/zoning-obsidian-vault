# Market Analysis Frameworks Index

## Overview

This folder contains the analytical frameworks used to study a real estate market or submarket in preparation for an acquisition, development feasibility analysis, or market study. The frameworks span the full arc of market research: defining the competitive geography, quantifying demand and supply, benchmarking rents and pricing, and profiling the demographic and behavioral characteristics of the consumer or tenant base. They are designed to be used in sequence — submarket definition first, demand and supply analysis second, comps and demographic analysis third — though individual frameworks can also be applied in isolation depending on the research question.

## Contents

- [[Submarket Definition and Selection]] — Framework for drawing defensible submarket boundaries and selecting the right competitive set for any CRE market study.
- [[Demand Driver Analysis]] — Framework for identifying, quantifying, and stress-testing the economic forces that generate occupancy demand for a specific property type in a defined submarket.
- [[Supply Pipeline Analysis]] — Framework for inventorying, phasing, and risk-adjusting all competitive new supply under construction or in permitting to forecast near-term market conditions.
- [[Comparable Sales (Sales Comps)]] — Framework for sourcing, screening, and adjusting comparable sales transactions to bracket a subject property's market value on a per-SF, per-unit, or cap rate basis.
- [[Comparable Rents (Rent Comps)]] — Framework for sourcing, adjusting, and reconciling comparable lease transactions to establish achievable market rents for a subject property.
- [[Demographic Analysis]] — Framework for profiling the population, household, age, income, and education characteristics of a trade area to support demand underwriting for residential and consumer-facing CRE.
- [[Employment and Jobs Analysis]] — Framework for analyzing local employment base, sector composition, growth trends, and labor supply to underpin demand projections for office, industrial, and other employment-driven CRE.
- [[Traffic Counts and Visibility]] — Framework for sourcing, interpreting, and applying roadway traffic count data and physical visibility metrics to evaluate commercial site quality and retail, hospitality, and QSR demand potential.
- [[Trade Area Analysis (Retail)]] — Framework for defining the geographic zone from which a retail property draws customers, quantifying spending potential, and measuring retail demand against existing supply to identify gaps or surplus.
- [[Psychographic Analysis]] — Framework for segmenting a trade area population by lifestyle, values, and consumer behavior patterns to refine tenant mix, product positioning, and marketing strategy for retail and multifamily properties.
