# Specialty Asset Classes Index

## Overview

This index covers the ten specialty CRE asset classes documented in `10-cre-brain/asset-classes/specialty/`. Each specialty is treated as a single overview note covering the full investment thesis, subtypes, demand drivers, supply dynamics, underwriting norms, key operating metrics, deal structures, risks, regulatory considerations, and notable markets. These asset classes share the characteristic that their investment and operating logic differs materially from the mainstream classes (Multifamily, Office, Retail, Industrial, Hospitality, Self-Storage, Mixed-Use, Ground-Up Development, Land) — each requires specialized operator relationships, regulatory knowledge, and underwriting expertise.

## Contents

- [[Data Center]] — Power-intensive facilities housing computing infrastructure for hyperscalers, enterprise tenants, and colocation users, underwritten on critical power capacity and lease structure.
- [[Life Sciences]] — Specialized laboratory and R&D facilities for biotech, pharmaceutical, and medical device tenants, underwritten on cluster proximity, wet-lab infrastructure, and research funding.
- [[Healthcare / Medical Office (MOB)]] — Purpose-built or adapted facilities for outpatient healthcare delivery, underwritten on health-system credit, on-campus vs. off-campus positioning, and long tenant switching costs.
- [[Senior Housing]] — Residential and care facilities for seniors across the continuum from independent living to skilled nursing, underwritten on care-level mix, RevPOR, labor cost ratio, and demographic tailwinds.
- [[Student Housing]] — Purpose-built or converted residential properties serving university students, underwritten on enrollment trends, distance-to-campus, and bed-level lease structures.
- [[Manufactured Housing]] — Land-lease communities where residents own manufactured homes and pay lot rent, offering highly stable NOI, low capital intensity, and workforce-housing demand from affordability pressures.
- [[Marina]] — Water-access facilities providing boat slips, dry storage, fuel, and marine services, underwritten on slip demand, revenue per slip, and the scarcity of coastal permitting.
- [[Parking Garage]] — Structured parking facilities generating revenue per stall from transient hourly users and contract monthly parkers, with long-term demand risk from autonomous vehicles and remote work.
- [[Religious Facilities]] — Worship spaces and ancillary campus buildings owned by faith communities, analyzed primarily through adaptive reuse, sale-leaseback, and declining-congregation disposition frameworks.
- [[Education Facilities]] — Schools, charter school campuses, early childhood centers, and educational buildings underwritten on enrollment stability, operator credit, net-lease structure, and mission-aligned capital sources.
