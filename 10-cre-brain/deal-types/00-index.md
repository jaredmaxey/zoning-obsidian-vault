# Deal Types Index

## Overview

This folder documents the canonical deal archetypes used in commercial real estate investing and development. Each note describes a distinct business plan — how it is capitalized, underwritten, what risks it carries, and how it typically exits. Notes are organized below from lowest to highest risk/return profile, followed by specialized structures that span multiple risk tiers. See [[CRE Brain Index]] for the full CRE brain map.

## Contents

### Core and Core-Plus Strategies

- [[Core Acquisition]] — Acquisition of stabilized, institutional-quality assets in primary markets targeting steady income with minimal execution risk and long hold periods.
- [[Core-Plus Acquisition]] — Acquisition of high-quality but slightly imperfect assets requiring modest leasing or light-renovation effort to achieve full stabilized income potential.

### Value-Add Strategies

- [[Value-Add Acquisition]] — Acquisition of an underperforming asset requiring significant capital investment, operational improvement, or re-leasing to unlock embedded NOI growth and equity appreciation.
- [[Recapitalization]] — Restructuring of an existing property's capital structure — debt, equity, or both — to provide liquidity to existing investors, reset the capital stack, or fund a new business plan phase.

### Opportunistic Strategies

- [[Opportunistic Acquisition]] — High-conviction acquisition of deeply distressed, complex, or market-dislocated assets demanding significant capital, expertise, and risk tolerance in pursuit of outsized equity returns.
- [[Ground-Up Development]] — New construction of commercial real estate from a bare or cleared site, requiring entitlement, design, financing, and delivery of a building that does not yet exist.
- [[Adaptive Reuse]] — Conversion of an existing building from its original use to a substantially different use, creating new value by repositioning an obsolete asset into a higher-demand product type.
- [[Distressed Acquisition]] — Purchase of an asset or its debt at a discount to intrinsic value due to ownership distress, loan default, or forced-sale conditions, targeting recovery and stabilization for outsized equity returns.
- [[Land Banking]] — Acquisition and patient holding of raw or minimally entitled land in the path of growth to profit from future appreciation driven by entitlement, infrastructure, or demographic expansion.
- [[Entitlement Play]] — Acquisition or control of land to actively pursue and obtain development entitlements, then exit by selling the entitled land or transitioning to construction for development profit.

### Specialized Structures

- [[Sale-Leaseback]] — Transaction in which a property owner sells its real estate to an investor and simultaneously leases it back, converting owned real estate into capital while retaining operational occupancy.
- [[Build-to-Suit]] — Development of a commercial building to a specific tenant's requirements under a pre-negotiated lease commitment, eliminating lease-up risk in exchange for tailored design and longer development management.
