# Tucson Parking Standards

## Summary

The Tucson Unified Development Code establishes citywide off-street parking requirements applicable to new development, changes of use, and expansions. The standards regulate minimum (and in some zones maximum) vehicle parking ratios by use type, parking lot design (dimensions, access, surfacing, and landscaping), ADA-accessible parking, motorcycle parking, and bicycle parking. Tucson's parking framework reflects the city's dual goals of functional car-oriented suburban development and increasing multimodal access in the urban core. The Rio Nuevo and certain transit-adjacent areas may have modified or reduced parking requirements.

Parking compliance must be demonstrated in the Development Package. Reductions from minimum requirements require a variance or, in some cases, a parking alternative plan. See [[Parking Ratios by Use]] for cross-jurisdictional comparison data.

## Applicability

Off-street parking standards apply to all new commercial, multifamily, mixed-use, and industrial development within Tucson city limits. Existing uses with a change of use that increases parking demand must meet current standards for the incremental increase. Single-family residential parking is separately addressed. Projects within certain overlay districts (e.g., Rio Nuevo, transit corridors) may qualify for reduced ratios. Specific exemptions and alternative compliance paths are TBD — verify against the current UDC parking article (`needs-verification`).

## Exceptions

Reductions from minimum parking ratios may be available through: (a) a shared parking agreement with adjacent uses; (b) proximity to transit (specific thresholds TBD — verify against UDC, `needs-verification`); (c) a Transportation Demand Management (TDM) plan; or (d) a variance before the Zoning Examiner demonstrating unique site hardship. Some zones or overlays in the urban core may apply maximum parking caps in addition to or instead of minimums.

## Calculation Examples

*Illustrative only — ratio values are TBD pending UDC verification.*

Example: A 20,000 SF retail center at a hypothetical ratio of 4 spaces per 1,000 SF GFA would require 80 vehicle spaces. With 5% of those being ADA-accessible, the project would need a minimum of 4 accessible spaces. Bicycle parking requirements would add additional spaces per the UDC table for retail uses.

Example: A 100-unit multifamily project with a mix of 1BR and 2BR units — required parking would be calculated per unit type at the applicable ratio, then summed. ADA and bicycle parking are additive requirements.
