# Tucson Walls and Fences Standards

## Summary

The Tucson UDC regulates the construction of walls and fences for commercial, industrial, and multifamily development in terms of height, materials, placement relative to setback lines and rights-of-way, and screening obligations. Wall and fence requirements commonly arise at the interface between commercial and residential uses — where screening walls are required to buffer parking, mechanical equipment, loading areas, and outdoor storage from adjacent residential zones. Walls and fence plans are a required component of the Development Package submittal.

In Tucson's desert context, masonry walls are the predominant fencing material for commercial projects, and block-and-plaster construction is common in both residential and commercial applications. The UDC may specify acceptable materials and finished appearances for fences visible from public rights-of-way.

## Applicability

Wall and fence standards apply to all new commercial, industrial, and multifamily development. Screening wall requirements are typically triggered at the boundary between incompatible uses (e.g., commercial parking adjacent to residential zones). Single-family residential fence standards are separately regulated. Projects in overlay districts (Historic Preservation Zone, Rio Nuevo) may have enhanced design standards for walls and fences visible from public rights-of-way.

## Exceptions

Variances for wall height or placement deviations may be obtained from the Zoning Examiner upon demonstration of hardship. Alternative screening plans using dense landscaping in lieu of a masonry wall may be accepted by PDS in certain circumstances. Projects on irregular lots or with existing adjacent structures may qualify for administrative adjustments.

## Related Zones

Screening wall requirements are most commonly triggered in commercial zone districts adjacent to residential zones. Projects within the Historic Preservation Zone may have masonry coursing, color, and finish requirements for walls visible from public streets.
