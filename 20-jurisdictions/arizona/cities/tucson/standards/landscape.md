# Tucson Landscape Standards

## Summary

Tucson's landscape standards are among the most detailed in Arizona, reflecting the city's desert environment, water conservation goals, and attention to urban heat island mitigation. The Tucson UDC landscape article requires native and low-water-use plant species, established coverage and canopy standards, passive water harvesting features, buffer yards between incompatible uses, and parking lot landscape islands. The standards are designed to reinforce Tucson's Sonoran Desert character while supporting livable, shaded development.

Landscape plans are required as part of every Development Package submittal. The city may also require a water harvesting plan demonstrating that a specified proportion of on-site runoff is directed to landscape areas before entering the stormwater system. Compliance is verified during Development Package review and final inspection.

## Applicability

Landscape standards apply to new commercial, multifamily, mixed-use, and industrial development, as well as major expansions triggering landscape upgrade requirements. Single-family residential landscape is addressed separately. Projects within designated overlay districts may have additional or modified landscape requirements. Specific thresholds for when landscape upgrade applies to expansions are TBD — verify against the current UDC landscape article (`needs-verification`).

## Exceptions

Modifications to landscape standards may be available through: (a) an alternative landscape plan demonstrating equivalent water conservation and aesthetic goals; (b) a variance before the Zoning Examiner; or (c) specific exemptions for constrained infill lots. Projects on previously developed sites (adaptive reuse or redevelopment) may have reduced landscape upgrade obligations. Specific relief mechanisms are TBD — verify against the UDC (`needs-verification`).

## Related Zones

Landscape standards apply across all commercial, multifamily, and industrial zone districts. Projects within the Historic Preservation Zone or Rio Nuevo overlay may have enhanced or alternative plant palette and design requirements.
