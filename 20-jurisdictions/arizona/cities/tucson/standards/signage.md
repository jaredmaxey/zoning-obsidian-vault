# Tucson Signage Standards

## Summary

The Tucson UDC regulates the type, size, height, number, placement, and illumination of signs for commercial, industrial, multifamily, and institutional uses throughout the city. Tucson's sign code reflects a balance between business visibility and the city's scenic and historic character — particularly in the downtown and historic neighborhoods where design guidelines impose stricter controls. Sign permits are required for most new signage and are administered by PDS.

Overlay districts — particularly the Historic Preservation Zone and Rio Nuevo — impose additional design review requirements for signage. Scenic corridor designations along major arterials may also restrict sign height and area beyond the base zone minimums.

## Applicability

Sign standards apply to all commercial, industrial, mixed-use, and multifamily development. Residential uses (single-family) have separate, more limited provisions. Projects within overlay districts must meet both the base UDC sign standards and any overlay-specific modifications. Sign permits are required prior to installation; administrative review by PDS staff is the typical path for standard permitted sign types.

## Exceptions

Sign variances may be sought before the Zoning Examiner for deviations from dimensional limits. Comprehensive sign programs (for multi-tenant centers) can be approved through an administrative process to allow uniform design flexibility. Historic Preservation Zone modifications require Historic Preservation Office review and may follow a separate administrative pathway.
