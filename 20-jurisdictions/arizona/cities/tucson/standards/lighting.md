# Tucson Lighting Standards

## Summary

Tucson has some of the most stringent outdoor lighting regulations in the United States, driven by the city's proximity to Kitt Peak National Observatory and its longstanding commitment to dark-sky preservation. The Tucson UDC outdoor lighting article — closely coordinated with the Pima County Outdoor Lighting Code — requires fully shielded, full-cutoff fixtures; limits allowable lamp types and color temperatures; caps light trespass at property boundaries; and restricts upward light emission. These standards are not merely aesthetic — they carry observatory-protection force and have shaped commercial site design in Tucson for decades.

Compliance is demonstrated through a photometric plan submitted as part of the Development Package. PDS reviews the plan for fixture type, maximum illumination levels (foot-candles), and light trespass at property lines. Noncompliant fixture specifications or insufficient shielding are common review comments.

## Applicability

Outdoor lighting standards apply to all new commercial, industrial, multifamily, and mixed-use development, as well as significant expansions that trigger lighting plan review. Parking lot lighting, building-mounted wall packs, canopy lighting, security lighting, and sign illumination are all regulated. The standards coordinate with Pima County's Outdoor Lighting Code; projects on the city/county boundary must satisfy both regimes. ## Exceptions

Lighting variances may be sought before the Zoning Examiner for deviations from specific foot-candle or fixture requirements. Because the dark-sky requirements carry observatory-protection intent, variances on core shielding or upward-light requirements are rare and difficult to obtain. Alternative lighting plans demonstrating equivalent dark-sky performance may be accepted by PDS on a case-by-case basis.

Example: A 100-space surface parking lot would require a photometric plan showing average maintained foot-candle levels at or below the UDC maximum, using full-cutoff pole-mounted fixtures. Foot-candle readings at the property line perimeter must stay within the trespass limit. The photometric plan's point-by-point grid would be reviewed by PDS against both the average and maximum criteria.

Example: A gas station canopy must use flush or recessed fixtures so that light is directed downward only, with zero upward spill. High-intensity discharge (HID) lamps in unsatisfied color-temperature categories may require replacement with LED fixtures meeting CCT restrictions.

## Related Zones

Outdoor lighting standards apply across all non-residential and multifamily zone districts. The Historic Preservation Zone and Rio Nuevo overlay may impose additional fixture design or aesthetic restrictions beyond the base UDC lighting code.
