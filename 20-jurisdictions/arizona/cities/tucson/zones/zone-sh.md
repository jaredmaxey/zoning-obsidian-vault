# Tucson SH

## Summary

The Suburban Homestead Zone (SH) occupies the middle tier of Tucson's low-density residential spectrum (UDC § 4.7.5). It is applied to established and developing suburban neighborhoods that are fully served by urban infrastructure. The SH zone is characterized by conventional single-family detached housing at moderate suburban densities — larger lots than the RX-series urban residential zones but smaller and more intensively developed than SR or RH. The SH designation is common in Tucson's mature suburban quadrants and in newer planned communities on the outer growth boundary.

## Overlay Interactions

SH zones in hillside or wash-adjacent locations may be subject to Hillside Development Zone (HDZ) or Environmental Resource Zone (ERZ) overlays. Verify on the Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new residential construction
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for day care, accessory dwelling units, and certain other uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to step up to RX-1, R-1, or higher-density zones
