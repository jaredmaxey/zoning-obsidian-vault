# Tucson OCR-1

## Summary

The Office/Commercial/Residential Zone OCR-1 (UDC § 4.7.26) is a high-intensity mixed-use zone designed to provide for high-rise or large-scale development that serves the community and region. According to UDC § 4.7.26, the zone is intended for major activity centers or transit centers where a mixture of development types is encouraged — including office, commercial, and high-density residential uses. OCR-1 is the lower-intensity of Tucson's two OCR zones and represents the transit-oriented, mixed-use development prototype appropriate for major intersections, transit hubs, and designated activity centers identified in Plan Tucson.

For CRE purposes, OCR-1 is the target zone for mixed-use tower, podium, and campus-scale developments combining office, high-density residential, and retail in transit-accessible locations.

## Subdivision Standards

OCR-1 development typically occurs on consolidated parcels with structured parking and phased mixed-use programs. Governed by UDC commercial and mixed-use development standards.

## Overlay Interactions

OCR-1 zones near downtown may be subject to the Downtown Area Infill Incentive District (DAIID), Rio Nuevo Overlay, and Urban Overlay District overlays, which can modify height, setback, and parking requirements. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new mixed-use construction (development package review through PDS)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for entertainment, cultural, or certain large-scale uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to OCR-2 for even higher-intensity development
