# Tucson OS

## Summary

The Open Space Zone (OS) is the most restrictive base zone district in the Tucson Unified Development Code (§ 4.7.1). It is applied to land intended to remain in permanent open space use — including public parks, natural preserves, riparian corridors, mountain park land, and environmentally sensitive terrain. The OS designation is used by the city to protect hillside areas, floodplains, the Santa Cruz River corridor, and dedicated park land from incompatible development. Intensity of use is extremely low; the zone is generally not appropriate for private commercial or residential development.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by citywide subdivision standards in the UDC. OS-zoned land is rarely subdivided for private development purposes.

## Overlay Interactions

OS zones may overlap with the Hillside Development Zone (HDZ) overlay, Environmental Resource Zone (ERZ) overlay, and the Scenic Corridor Zone overlay. Each overlay may impose additional development restrictions. Verify applicable overlays on the Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

Development within the OS zone is extremely limited. Any proposal that could be permitted would typically require:

- [[Site Plan Review]] for infrastructure or utility work
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for any non-passive use
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] if a use intensification is sought

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson IR]] — Institutional Reserve, similarly low-intensity
- [[Tucson RH]] — Rural Homestead, adjacent in low-density fringe areas
- [[Tucson SR]] — Suburban Ranch, typical transition at urban edge
