# Tucson SR

## Summary

The Suburban Ranch Zone (SR) is the second-lowest-density residential base zone in the Tucson UDC (§ 4.7.4). It is applied to areas transitioning from rural to suburban character — typically found in the outer suburban ring where city services are available but lower densities are desired. The SR zone allows single-family detached housing on large lots together with limited agricultural and equestrian uses. It represents an intermediate step between the fully rural RH zone and the conventional suburban SH and RX-series residential zones.

For CRE purposes, SR land is most relevant for low-density residential subdivision or estate development. Rezoning to higher-density residential or commercial zones is possible with demonstrated Plan Tucson consistency.

## Overlay Interactions

SR zones may be subject to Hillside Development Zone (HDZ), Environmental Resource Zone (ERZ), or Scenic Corridor Zone overlays depending on location. Verify on the Tucson GIS zoning map.
