# Tucson R-1

## Summary

The Residence Zone R-1 (UDC § 4.7.8) is the primary conventional urban single-family residential zone in Tucson's UDC. It is one of the most widely applied base zones across Tucson's established neighborhoods, applied to areas with standard urban lot sizes, full infrastructure, and single-family detached residential character. R-1 is the baseline zone for most infill residential development in Tucson's inner and mid-ring neighborhoods.

For CRE purposes, R-1 is the standard single-family infill zone. Multifamily, commercial, and industrial uses require rezoning. R-1 land is a common target for lot assemblage and rezoning to R-2, R-3, or commercial zones in areas designated for higher density in Plan Tucson.

## Subdivision Standards

Standard urban residential subdivision governed by UDC. Infill lot splits may be processed administratively depending on size and configuration.

## Overlay Interactions

R-1 zones may be subject to Historic Preservation Zone overlay in older Tucson neighborhoods (Barrio, Sam Hughes, Armory Park, etc.). Urban Overlay District and other infill overlays may also apply depending on location.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new residential construction
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for day care and certain accessory uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to step up to R-2, R-3, MH-1, or commercial zones
