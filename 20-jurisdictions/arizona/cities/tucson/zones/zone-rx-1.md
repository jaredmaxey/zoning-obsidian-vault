# Tucson RX-1

## Summary

The Residence Zone RX-1 (UDC § 4.7.6) is the first of two urban transition residential zones in the Tucson UDC, situated between the larger-lot Suburban Homestead (SH) zone and the fully urban R-1 Residence Zone. RX-1 is applied to established Tucson neighborhoods and newer infill areas that have conventional urban single-family character. Lot sizes are smaller than SH but generally larger than R-1, and the zone is typically served by full urban infrastructure. The RX-series zones bridge the suburban and urban residential ladders in Tucson's zoning framework.

For CRE purposes, RX-1 is a standard infill single-family residential zone. Multifamily and commercial development requires rezoning.

## Subdivision Standards

Conventional urban residential subdivision governed by UDC.

## Overlay Interactions

RX-1 zones in hillside or wash-adjacent locations may be subject to Hillside Development Zone (HDZ) or Environmental Resource Zone (ERZ) overlays.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new residential construction
