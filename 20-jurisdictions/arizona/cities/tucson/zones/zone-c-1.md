# Tucson C-1

## Summary

The Commercial Zone C-1 (UDC § 4.7.20) is Tucson's primary general retail and service commercial zone. It is applied extensively along arterial corridors and at major intersections throughout the city, accommodating a broad range of retail, restaurant, service, and office uses in community- and neighborhood-scale formats. C-1 is the workhorse commercial zone in Tucson, representing the typical strip mall, mid-scale shopping center, and arterial retail corridor context. Uses are more intensive and diverse than the NC or RVC zones but less so than the C-2 or C-3 general commercial and highway commercial zones.

For CRE purposes, C-1 is the dominant zone for retail strip centers, mixed retail/service pads, and corridor commercial investment throughout Tucson.

## Subdivision Standards

Commercial lot subdivision governed by UDC. Many C-1 projects develop on single commercial-sized lots.

## Overlay Interactions

C-1 zones along major arterials are commonly subject to Gateway Corridor Zone, Scenic Corridor Zone, Major Streets and Routes Setback Zone, and Airport Environs Zone overlays depending on location. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new commercial construction (development package review)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for hotel, drive-through, and automotive uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to C-2 or C-3 for more intensive commercial uses
