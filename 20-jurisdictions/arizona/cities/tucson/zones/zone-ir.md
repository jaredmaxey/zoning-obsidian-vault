# Tucson IR

## Summary

The Institutional Reserve (IR) zone (UDC § 4.7.2) is applied to large institutional land holdings that serve community-wide or regional functions — including the University of Arizona campus, Davis-Monthan Air Force Base, major medical center campuses, and large government facilities. The zone is intended to protect the long-term institutional use of land and to allow campus-scale development in a coordinated manner while buffering impacts on surrounding residential neighborhoods. Development standards reflect campus-scale rather than parcel-by-parcel planning.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by citywide subdivision standards. IR-zoned campuses are typically held in single ownership and rarely platted into individual lots.

## Overlay Interactions

IR-zoned areas may be subject to Airport Environs Zone overlays (DMAFB or TIA environs) and other city-wide overlays depending on location. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new buildings or significant site changes within institutional campus
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for ancillary commercial or residential uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] if the institutional use ceases and a change in use is proposed

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson OS]] — Open Space, often adjacent to large campuses
- [[Tucson O-1]] — Office Zone, compatible transition to surrounding areas
- [[Tucson R-1]] — Residential zones often border institutional campuses
