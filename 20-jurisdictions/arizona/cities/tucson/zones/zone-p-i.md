# Tucson P-I

## Summary

The Park Industrial Zone P-I (UDC § 4.7.28) provides for corporate business centers, wholesaling and manufacturing activities, and select other uses that can be conducted in an unobtrusive, controlled manner. According to UDC § 4.7.28, the zone is intended to accommodate corporate business centers, select agricultural, civic, commercial, industrial, retail, storage, utility, and wholesaling uses that can be carried on in a controlled, campus-like environment. The P-I zone emphasizes high-quality site design, landscaping, and architectural standards to ensure compatibility with adjacent uses. It is typically found in master-planned business parks and technology/innovation districts.

For CRE purposes, P-I is the primary zone for flex industrial, R&D, corporate campus, and light manufacturing investment in Tucson. Sites are frequently larger, with higher landscape standards and more restrictive design requirements than I-1 zones.

## Subdivision Standards

Park industrial projects typically develop under master subdivision or condominium frameworks. Lot sizes tend to be larger than I-1. Governed by UDC.

## Overlay Interactions

P-I zones near Tucson International Airport (TIA) or Davis-Monthan AFB are commonly subject to Airport Environs Zone overlays and compatible use restrictions. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new industrial/business park construction (development package review)

- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to I-1 for standard light industrial uses without campus design requirements
