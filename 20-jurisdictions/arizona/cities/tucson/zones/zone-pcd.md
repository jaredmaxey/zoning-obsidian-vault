# Tucson PCD

## Summary

The Planned Community Development (PCD) zone (UDC §§ 4.7.25 and 3.5.6) is designed for large-scale master-planned community developments that integrate a comprehensive mix of residential, commercial, employment, civic, parks, and open space uses on a larger land assemblage. PCDs are the most comprehensive planned development tool in the Tucson UDC, typically applied to large greenfield sites or major mixed-use redevelopment projects where a coordinated development program — including multiple land uses, phasing, and internal infrastructure — is needed. Like PADs, PCD zoning is established through a legislative rezoning process before the Mayor and Council, and the adopted PCD document becomes the governing regulatory framework for the subject property.

PCDs differ from PADs primarily in scale and comprehensiveness — a PCD typically encompasses an entire planned community with multiple land-use zones, master infrastructure plans, and phased development programs.

Permitted uses are established on a PCD-by-PCD basis through the approved PCD document. A PCD typically permits a range of residential types, commercial, office, civic, recreational, and open space uses. Verify against the specific PCD ordinance.

## Subdivision Standards

PCDs typically incorporate a master subdivision plan as part of the development document. Large PCDs are phased over many years and involve multiple subdivision plats.

## Overlay Interactions

A PCD may incorporate or supersede overlay zone requirements. Verify the specific PCD document for overlay interaction language.

## Process & Approvals Required

Establishing PCD zoning requires:
- Pre-application meeting with PDS
- Mayor and Council legislative hearing (rezoning) — see [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]]
- Planning Commission public hearing and recommendation
- Comprehensive PCD master development plan, development text, and phasing program

Subsequent development within an approved PCD may require:
- [[Site Plan Review]] (development package review) for individual phases or lots
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] if specified in the PCD document

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson PAD]] — Planned Area Development (smaller-scale planned development)
- Base zones at the PCD boundary will vary by location

## Recent Amendments

PCD process standards are in UDC § 3.5.6. Individual PCDs are amended by separate ordinance. Verify the specific PCD ordinance for the subject property.
