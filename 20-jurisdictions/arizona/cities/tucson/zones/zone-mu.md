# Tucson MU

## Summary

The Mixed Use Zone (MU) (UDC § 4.7.23) is Tucson's dedicated horizontal and vertical mixed-use zone. It is designed to encourage the co-location of residential, commercial, and office uses in pedestrian-friendly, walkable formats at neighborhood centers, transit nodes, and infill corridors. The MU zone is a "special use zone" in the UDC's organizational framework (use table § 4.8.9: NC, RVC, MU) and typically requires active ground-floor commercial with residential above, or a mix of commercial and residential uses on the same site. The zone is commonly applied in the downtown, midtown, and transit corridor contexts where Plan Tucson promotes mixed-use infill and urban density.

For CRE purposes, MU is the primary zone for mixed-use infill development in Tucson — vertically mixed residential/retail/office buildings, live-work projects, and walkable neighborhood commercial nodes with upper-story housing.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by UDC. Mixed-use infill projects often develop on a single parcel or consolidated parcels.

## Overlay Interactions

MU zones frequently overlap with the Downtown Area Infill Incentive District (DAIID), Rio Nuevo Overlay, Urban Overlay District, and transit corridor overlays — each of which may modify standards or provide development bonuses. Verify applicable overlays on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new mixed-use construction (development package review)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for hotel and certain other uses
