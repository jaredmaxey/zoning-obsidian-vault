# Tucson R-3

## Summary

The Residence Zone R-3 (UDC § 4.7.12) is Tucson's high-density multifamily residential zone, intended for apartment buildings, townhouses, large residential developments, and urban housing at significant density. R-3 is applied to major corridors, transit nodes, the University District, and areas designated for higher-density housing in Plan Tucson. It accommodates a full range of residential building types from single-family detached through mid-rise multifamily, making it the workhorse zone for apartment and condo development in Tucson.

For CRE purposes, R-3 is the primary target zone for multifamily investment and development — both garden-style apartments and mid-density urban residential. Commercial uses are typically not permitted by-right; ancillary or ground-floor commercial may require a CUP or rezoning to a mixed-use zone.

## Subdivision Standards

Urban residential subdivision governed by UDC. Multifamily projects may be developed on a single lot without subdivision.

## Overlay Interactions

R-3 zones along major corridors may be subject to Gateway Corridor Zone, Scenic Corridor Zone, or Urban Overlay District overlays. Historic Preservation Zone applies in designated historic neighborhoods. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new multifamily construction (development package review through PDS)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for certain accessory commercial or institutional uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to add ground-floor commercial via MU or OCR zones
