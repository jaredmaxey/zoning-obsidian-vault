# Tucson PAD

## Summary

The Planned Area Development (PAD) zone (UDC §§ 4.7.24 and 3.5.5) is a customized zoning designation established through a legislative rezoning process before the Mayor and Council. Unlike base zones with fixed use tables and dimensional standards, a PAD is tailored to a specific site — the applicant submits a development plan, text, and standards that, if approved, become the governing regulations for that property. PADs are identified on the city zoning map by "PAD" followed by a number (e.g., PAD-1), with each PAD representing its own adopted set of development regulations.

PADs are used for larger, complex projects where a single base zone cannot adequately accommodate the intended development program, or where the developer seeks flexibility from standard dimensional requirements in exchange for public benefits or superior design. Common PAD types include large master-planned residential communities, mixed-use urban infill projects, resort/hospitality complexes, and employment campus developments.

## Permitted Uses

Permitted uses are established on a PAD-by-PAD basis through the approved PAD document. A PAD may permit any combination of residential, commercial, office, industrial, civic, hospitality, and mixed uses — or restrict uses to a narrower program. There is no universal PAD use table; verify against the specific PAD ordinance for the subject property.

## Subdivision Standards

Subdivision standards are established within the PAD document. Large PADs typically include a master subdivision or phasing plan.

## Overlay Interactions

A PAD may be layered with overlay zones. Overlay requirements may be incorporated into or superseded by the PAD document — verify the specific PAD for overlay interaction language.

## Process & Approvals Required

Obtaining PAD zoning requires:
- Pre-application meeting with PDS
- Mayor and Council legislative hearing (rezoning) — see [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]]
- Planning Commission public hearing and recommendation
- PAD development plan and text submitted and adopted by ordinance

Subsequent development within an approved PAD may require:
- [[Site Plan Review]] (development package review)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] if specified in the PAD document

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson PCD]] — Planned Community Development (larger-scale planned development counterpart)
- Any base zone may be adjacent; PADs are site-specific and context-dependent

## Recent Amendments

PAD process standards are in UDC § 3.5.5. Individual PADs are amended by separate ordinance. Verify the specific PAD ordinance for the subject property.
