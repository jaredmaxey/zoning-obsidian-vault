# Tucson RVC

## Summary

The Rural Village Center Zone (RVC) (UDC § 4.7.19) is designed for small-scale commercial activity within rural and low-density areas of Tucson's incorporated territory. The zone accommodates neighborhood-serving retail, services, and community uses appropriate to rural village settings — typically located at crossroads or entry points to rural residential areas served by RH or SR zoning. The RVC zone is the rural counterpart to the urban Neighborhood Commercial (NC) zone, with standards tailored to the lower densities and infrastructure levels of rural areas.

For CRE purposes, RVC opportunities are limited in scale and represent neighborhood convenience-commercial investment in Tucson's outlying rural areas. Like NC, it is covered in UDC use table § 4.8.9 (Special Use Zones: NC, RVC, MU).
