# Tucson OCR-2

## Summary

The Office/Commercial/Residential Zone OCR-2 (UDC § 4.7.27) is the highest-intensity mixed-use zone in the Tucson UDC. It is designed for large-scale, high-rise development at regional activity centers and major transit hubs, where the greatest concentration of office, commercial, and high-density residential uses is appropriate. OCR-2 allows even greater intensity than OCR-1 and is typically applied to the most prominent activity center nodes — downtown Tucson and major employment/transit hubs identified in the Plan Tucson future land-use map.

For CRE purposes, OCR-2 represents Tucson's highest-intensity urban development scenario — high-rise office towers, large hotel complexes, podium apartment buildings, and large mixed-use campus development.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by UDC. Large mixed-use campus development may involve lot consolidation, condominium platting, or phased development agreements.

## Overlay Interactions

OCR-2 zones are most likely found in the downtown and major activity center areas subject to DAIID (Downtown Area Infill Incentive District), Rio Nuevo Overlay, and Urban Overlay District, each of which may modify height, parking, setback, and design requirements. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new high-intensity mixed-use construction
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for entertainment, cultural, and large-scale uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] typically required to obtain OCR-2 on non-downtown sites
