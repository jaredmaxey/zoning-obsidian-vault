# Tucson RH

## Summary

The Rural Homestead Zone (RH) is the lowest-density residential base zone in the Tucson UDC (§ 4.7.3). It is applied to the most rural portions of the city's planning area to preserve rural and agricultural character and to encourage orderly growth of outlying areas. The zone accommodates very low-density single-family residential uses on large lots, together with small-scale agricultural activities and equestrian uses. It serves as a transitional designation between agricultural county land and the urbanizing suburban fringe. RH-zoned land is typically found in the far northwest, northeast, and southeast portions of Tucson's incorporated area, often near mountain park land or where water infrastructure is limited.

For CRE purposes, RH land is appropriate for rural residential subdivision, equestrian estates, or hobby farm projects, but is not appropriate for commercial or high-density residential investment without a rezoning.

## Subdivision Standards

Large-lot rural subdivision governed by UDC subdivision standards. Minimum lot sizes are very large — verify exact minimums against TABLE 6.3-1.A and the UDC subdivision chapter.

## Overlay Interactions

RH land may be subject to the Hillside Development Zone (HDZ) overlay in mountain-adjacent locations, Environmental Resource Zone (ERZ) overlay near washes and riparian areas, and Scenic Corridor Zone overlay. Verify applicable overlays on the Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new residential construction
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for certain accessory uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] required to intensify to SR, SH, or urban residential zones
- [[Planned Unit Development (PUD)]] via PAD process may be available for larger assemblages

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson SR]] — Suburban Ranch, the next step up in residential density
- [[Tucson OS]] — Open Space, commonly adjacent in mountain and park buffer areas
- [[Tucson IR]] — Institutional Reserve, may abut large government or military land
