# Tucson I-1

## Summary

The Light Industrial Zone I-1 (UDC § 4.7.29) is Tucson's standard light industrial zone, designed for manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, wholesale trade, and industrial service uses that do not generate significant nuisance effects such as heavy noise, noxious emissions, or extensive outdoor storage. I-1 is applied to established industrial parks, logistics clusters near TIA and the Interstate 10 corridor, and areas designated for employment and industrial land uses in Plan Tucson. The zone is more permissive than P-I with respect to use range but maintains standards to limit impacts on adjacent zones.

For CRE purposes, I-1 is the primary zone for warehouse/distribution, flex industrial, and light manufacturing investment in Tucson's industrial submarket. Demand drivers include airport proximity, I-10 access, and growing logistics and e-commerce distribution activity.

## Subdivision Standards

Industrial subdivision governed by UDC. Multi-tenant industrial parks commonly use condominium or lot-based subdivision.

## Overlay Interactions

I-1 zones near TIA are commonly subject to Airport Environs Zone (AEZ) overlay and compatible use restrictions. DMAFB Approach Departure Corridors (ADC) may affect certain I-1 areas. Verify on Tucson GIS zoning map.

## Process & Approvals Required

- [[Site Plan Review]] for new industrial construction (development package review)
- [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] for truck terminals, outdoor storage, and certain hazardous uses
- [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] to I-2 for heavy industrial uses; to P-I for campus industrial

## Adjacent / Compatible Zones

- [[Tucson P-I]] — Park Industrial (campus industrial, lower nuisance standard)
- [[Tucson I-2]] — Heavy Industrial (step-up for more intensive manufacturing)
- [[Tucson C-3]] — Commercial Zone (commercial transition at industrial edge)
