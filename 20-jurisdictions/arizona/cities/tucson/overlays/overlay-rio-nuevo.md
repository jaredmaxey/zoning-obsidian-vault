# Tucson Rio Nuevo Overlay

## Summary

The Rio Nuevo district encompasses Tucson's downtown core and is the primary focus of the city's urban redevelopment strategy. Rio Nuevo is both a state-authorized Multipurpose Facilities District (MFD) — with its own board and tax-increment financing authority — and the subject of specific UDC zoning overlay provisions that modify development standards and approval processes for the district. The overlay supports higher-density, mixed-use, pedestrian-oriented development consistent with downtown revitalization goals. For CRE developers, Rio Nuevo is significant because it offers potential tax-increment financing incentives, modified parking requirements (typically reduced or waived for certain development types), and a governance structure that can facilitate complex projects on difficult infill sites.

## Geographic Extent

The Rio Nuevo district boundary covers Tucson's downtown core, generally bounded by the railroad corridor, I-10, and the historic barrio neighborhoods to the south and east. The exact boundary is mapped by the Rio Nuevo Multipurpose Facilities District and the city's GIS system. - **Use mix**: Broader permitted use mix than many base zones, supporting mixed-income housing, retail, hospitality, office, and entertainment uses within the same structure or district.
- **Design standards**: Active frontage, ground-floor activation, and pedestrian-scale design requirements may apply in lieu of or in addition to base zone setback and landscaping standards.

## Additional Requirements

Projects within the Rio Nuevo district may require Rio Nuevo Board review and approval in addition to PDS Development Package review, particularly for projects seeking tax-increment financing or other district incentives. Design compatibility with the evolving downtown streetscape character is expected. Projects involving historic resources within the Rio Nuevo boundary also require Historic Preservation Office review (see [[Tucson Historic Preservation Zone]]).

## Process Changes

The approval path for Rio Nuevo projects may involve: (a) Rio Nuevo Board pre-application consultation and review for incentive eligibility; (b) standard PDS Development Package review; (c) concurrent Historic Preservation Office review if applicable; and (d) potential Council action for development agreements or incentive packages.
