# Tucson Historic Preservation Zone

## Summary

The Historic Preservation Zone (HPZ) is a Tucson overlay designation applied to areas and individual properties of recognized historic, architectural, or cultural significance. Tucson has a rich inventory of historic resources — including downtown commercial buildings, 19th- and early 20th-century barrio neighborhoods (El Barrio Histórico, Barrio Viejo), bungalow districts, and mid-century modern commercial corridors. The HPZ overlay imposes design review requirements on exterior alterations, new construction within designated districts, and demolition, with the goal of preserving the character-defining features of historic resources. The Historic Preservation Office (HPO), within PDS, administers the HPZ program. HPO review is required in addition to, and concurrent with, Development Package review for projects within HPZ boundaries.

## Geographic Extent

HPZ boundaries are mapped and maintained by the Historic Preservation Office. Designated districts include parts of downtown Tucson, Barrio Histórico, Armory Park, and other historic neighborhoods. Individual landmark designations may also trigger HPZ review on non-district properties. Parcel-level HPZ applicability should be verified through the official zoning map and HPO records before any pre-application planning. ## Modifications to Base Zoning

The HPZ overlay adds design review requirements and demolition controls on top of the base zone's use and dimensional standards; it typically does not relax base zone dimensional standards. Key modifications include:

- **Exterior alterations**: Changes to facades, windows, doors, rooflines, and character-defining features visible from public rights-of-way require HPO review and approval (Certificate of Appropriateness or similar — verify exact instrument against current UDC, `needs-verification`).
- **New construction within a district**: New buildings must be compatible with the historic character of the district in terms of massing, scale, materials, rhythm, and setbacks.
- **Demolition of contributing structures**: Demolition of historic resources requires heightened review and findings; demolition by neglect policies may also apply. Specific demolition findings are TBD — verify against the UDC and HPO procedures (`needs-verification`).

## Additional Requirements

Applicants for development within an HPZ should expect: (a) pre-application consultation with the Historic Preservation Office; (b) preparation of a historic assessment or existing conditions documentation; (c) submission of architectural drawings demonstrating design compatibility; and (d) HPO review concurrent with Development Package review. Projects requiring approval of a Certificate of Appropriateness must schedule HPO review as part of the overall project timeline. The Tucson-Pima County Historical Commission may also have an advisory role for significant projects (`needs-verification`).

## Process Changes

HPO review runs concurrently with PDS Development Package review. For projects requiring a Certificate of Appropriateness, HPO issues its determination separately from the Development Package approval; both must be obtained before permits are issued. Appeals of HPO determinations follow a separate administrative path — verify against the UDC and HPO procedures (`needs-verification`).
