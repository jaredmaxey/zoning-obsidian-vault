# Prescott Valley, Arizona

## Overview

Prescott Valley is a Yavapai County town located immediately east of Prescott along State Route 69 in the central Arizona highlands. It is the largest community in the "Quad Cities" area (Prescott, Prescott Valley, Chino Valley, and Dewey-Humboldt) by population, having grown rapidly since incorporation in the 1970s as a more affordable alternative to Prescott proper.

The town is primarily residential in character, but its SR 69 commercial corridor is the primary retail spine for the entire Quad Cities area, hosting major retail anchors, grocery stores, restaurants, and service commercial. This regional retail role gives Prescott Valley a stronger commercial CRE profile than its bedroom-community origins might suggest. The Findlay Toyota Center arena anchors a events and entertainment presence.

For CRE, Prescott Valley is the most active growth market in the Quad Cities area. Residential [[Subdivision Approval]] activity is ongoing, and retail and service commercial development follows population growth. Healthcare demand is significant given regional demographics. Large-format industrial is limited by the high-elevation terrain and distance from major logistics corridors.

## Governing Body

Prescott Valley operates as a town under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the Town Council makes final decisions on rezonings and general plan amendments.
