# Flagstaff, Arizona

## Overview

Flagstaff is the county seat of Coconino County and Arizona's largest high-elevation city, situated at approximately 7,000 feet above sea level along I-40 and historic Route 66 in Northern Arizona. Northern Arizona University (NAU) is the dominant economic institution, making Flagstaff a university town with all the attendant CRE implications: student housing demand, university-adjacent retail, and a research/technology sector tied to the university.

The city's CRE market is distinctively supply-constrained. Flagstaff is largely surrounded by Coconino National Forest, the Arizona Snowbowl ski resort land, and other federally managed lands that cannot be developed — severely limiting the land base available for new construction. This constraint drives up land values and makes infill, adaptive reuse, and higher-density redevelopment the dominant development strategies. Environmental sensitivity and water availability are recurring entitlement concerns.

Tourism (Route 66, Grand Canyon gateway, Snowbowl skiing, astronomy/dark-sky designation) sustains a hospitality sector above what the local population base alone would support. For CRE, the primary plays are student/multifamily housing, hospitality, retail serving tourism and the university, and medical/professional office. Industrial is limited and typically small-format. Any [[Highest and Best Use Analysis]] must front-load the supply-constraint reality.

## Governing Body

Flagstaff operates under a Mayor-Council government with a professional City Manager. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings, general plan amendments, and major entitlements. Flagstaff has been an active proponent of housing supply reform given its affordability crisis.
