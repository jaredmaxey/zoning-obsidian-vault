# Phoenix R1-6

## Summary

The R1-6 district is Phoenix's smallest standard single-family residential zone, designed to accommodate detached single-family dwellings on lots with a minimum size of 6,000 square feet (per zone designation; verify). This district is typically applied in established and newer suburban neighborhoods where a moderately compact single-family character is desired. The intent is to provide a stable, low-density residential environment protecting the single-family character of neighborhoods while allowing standard accessory uses and limited community facilities.

The R1-6 designation is the most permissive of Phoenix's R1-x series in terms of minimum lot size, enabling higher single-family density than larger R1-x counterparts. It is frequently found in infill areas, post-WWII suburban tracts, and neighborhoods close to commercial corridors.

## Process & Approvals Required

- **By-right development** (conforming single-family): building permit through [[Site Plan Review]] (administrative).
- **Conditional uses** (schools, churches, day care): [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]] required — public hearing before Zoning Adjustment Hearing Officer or Planning Commission.
- **Dimensional relief**: [[Variance]] before the Zoning Adjustment Hearing Officer.
- **Rezoning**: [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] with Planning Commission recommendation and City Council approval.
