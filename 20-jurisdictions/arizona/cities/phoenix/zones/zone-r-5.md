# Phoenix R-5

## Summary

The R-5 district is Phoenix's highest-density base residential zone, intended for large-scale multifamily development — including mid-rise and high-rise apartment buildings — in locations that can support maximum residential intensity. R-5 is typically applied in the downtown core, near major employment centers, along key transit corridors, and in urban infill contexts targeted for significant residential densification.

The district's purpose is to enable the most intensive form of residential development within the city's residential zoning framework, promoting efficient land use in well-served urban locations. R-5 development often involves structured parking, ground-floor amenity space, and multi-story construction. It sits at the top of Phoenix's residential zoning hierarchy and may interface with the Downtown Code or TOD overlay in parts of the city.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by citywide subdivision standards — see [[Phoenix, Arizona]] and the Phoenix Subdivision Ordinance. High-rise projects often require lot consolidation and may involve complex utility and easement negotiations.

## Overlay Interactions

- [[Phoenix Downtown Code]]: may apply in the downtown core, modifying height, massing, setbacks, and use standards for R-5 parcels.
- [[Phoenix Transit-Oriented Development Overlay]]: may increase density allowances and reduce or eliminate parking minimums near light-rail stations.
- [[Phoenix Historic Preservation Overlay]]: may restrict demolitions and require design review in designated historic areas.
- [[Phoenix Hillside Overlay]]: unlikely in typical R-5 urban contexts, but verify for hillside-adjacent sites.
