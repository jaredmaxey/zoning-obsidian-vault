# Phoenix Transit-Oriented Development Overlay

## Summary

The Phoenix Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) Overlay is applied to areas within defined walking distances of light rail stations and other high-capacity transit stops on the Valley Metro network. Its purpose is to encourage compact, mixed-use, pedestrian-oriented development that maximizes ridership and reduces auto-dependency, consistent with Phoenix's General Plan policies and the regional transit investment in the Valley Metro Light Rail and future extensions.

The TOD overlay modifies the underlying base zone by permitting higher densities, reducing or eliminating minimum parking requirements, establishing streetscape and design standards that prioritize pedestrian movement, and allowing a broader mix of uses than the base zone may otherwise allow. The TOD overlay applies within defined station-area boundaries — typically within a half-mile or quarter-mile walking radius of a light rail station, though the exact radius and boundary geometry vary by station and are mapped on the official Phoenix zoning map. ## Process Changes

Most projects in the TOD overlay follow the standard Phoenix site plan and building permit process, with the overlay standards applied as additional criteria. Key process considerations:

1. Pre-application consultation with Phoenix Planning and Development to confirm overlay sub-zone, applicable standards, and any required studies.
2. Site plan review incorporating TOD overlay standards for parking, design, and pedestrian connectivity. See [[Phoenix Site Plan Review]].
3. If a project requires a use permit or rezoning (e.g., a use not by-right in the TOD overlay), those processes apply. See [[Phoenix Use Permit / CUP]] and [[Phoenix Rezoning]].
