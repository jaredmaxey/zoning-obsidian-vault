# Phoenix Historic Preservation Overlay

## Summary

The Phoenix Historic Preservation Overlay is a zoning overlay district applied to designated historic districts and to individually-landmarked properties throughout the city. Its purpose is to protect the historic, architectural, and cultural character of Phoenix's older neighborhoods and significant structures by subjecting exterior alterations, new construction, and demolition within the overlay boundary to additional design review. The overlay works in tandem with the underlying base zone — base-zone dimensional and use regulations remain in effect, but projects that would visibly affect the historic character of a resource must satisfy the overlay's compatibility standards before a permit may issue.

Phoenix maintains a locally designated Historic Preservation program, separate from and potentially in addition to listing on the National Register of Historic Places. Local designation triggers the overlay's regulatory requirements. The Historic Preservation Officer and, for larger decisions, the Historic Preservation Commission play a central role in administering the overlay.

## Modifications to Base Zoning

The overlay does not typically alter the permitted uses, dimensional envelope, or density of the underlying base zone. Instead, it imposes a compatibility review layer on top of base-zone permissions:

- **Exterior alterations and additions:** Work that is visible from a public right-of-way on a contributing or individually-designated structure requires a Certificate of Appropriateness (or equivalent approval) demonstrating compatibility with the historic character. ## Process Changes

Projects within the Historic Preservation Overlay that require a COA must complete that review concurrently with or prior to submitting for a building permit. The typical process adds a design-review step:

1. Pre-application consultation with the Phoenix Historic Preservation Officer (recommended and sometimes required).
2. Submission of a COA application with drawings, material samples, and a narrative demonstrating compatibility.
3. Staff-level COA review for minor work; Historic Preservation Commission review for major alterations, new construction, or demolition proposals. TBD — confirm staff vs. commission delegation thresholds.
4. Public hearing may be required for Commission-level decisions. TBD — confirm hearing requirements.
5. COA issued (or denied), then building permit application proceeds.
