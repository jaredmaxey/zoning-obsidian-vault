# Phoenix Signage Standards

## Summary

The Phoenix Signage Standards (Sign Code) regulate the size, height, placement, type, illumination, and permitting of signs on all properties in the city. The sign code applies to on-premise signs (identifying a business or use at that location) and regulates off-premise advertising signs (billboards) separately. The standards balance business identification needs against visual character, traffic safety, and community aesthetics goals. Different zones permit different sign types and allow different maximum sign areas and heights — commercial and industrial zones allow larger and more prominent signage than residential zones.

The sign code is typically adopted as a standalone chapter of the Phoenix Zoning Ordinance or City Code. Phoenix's sign regulations also interact with First Amendment content-neutrality requirements established by case law (Reed v. Town of Gilbert and progeny), meaning sign regulations must generally be content-neutral and apply based on sign type/location rather than message. ## Applicability

The signage standards apply to:
- All new signs requiring a permit citywide.
- Modifications to or replacement of existing permitted signs above defined thresholds.
- Certain sign types (temporary signs, A-frame signs, real estate signs) that may be allowed by permit or by right with conditions.
- Billboards and off-premise advertising structures (typically subject to separate billboard ordinance provisions and state-level regulations). TBD — confirm billboard provisions.
- Signs in the [[Phoenix Historic Preservation Overlay]] are subject to additional design review requirements for compatibility with the historic character.

Exempt signs (typically small, temporary, or limited-duration signs) are defined in the code and do not require a permit. TBD — verify exempt sign categories and size thresholds.

## Exceptions

- **Nonconforming signs:** Legally permitted signs that become nonconforming due to code changes may continue as nonconforming structures but are typically subject to amortization schedules or loss of nonconforming status upon abandonment, damage, or reconstruction above defined thresholds. TBD — verify nonconforming sign provisions.
- **Governmental/public signs:** Traffic control signs, public information signs, and regulatory signs are typically exempt from the sign code.
- **Mural programs:** Phoenix may have a public art mural program permitting large artistic murals that would otherwise exceed sign area limits. TBD — confirm mural program provisions and whether it intersects with the sign code.
- **Variance:** A variance from sign code standards may be available through the [[Phoenix Variance]] process for hardship situations.
- **Special sign district:** Some areas may have adopted special sign district standards through the zoning process that supersede or supplement the base sign code. TBD — confirm existence and locations of any special sign districts.
