# Phoenix Parking Standards

## Summary

The Phoenix Parking Standards establish minimum off-street parking requirements for new development, changes of use, and expansions throughout the city. The standards apply citywide under the Phoenix Zoning Ordinance but are modified — and often reduced or eliminated — within the [[Phoenix Transit-Oriented Development Overlay]] and the [[Phoenix Downtown Code]] boundary. The purpose of minimum parking requirements is to ensure that development generates sufficient on-site parking to avoid spillover impacts on neighboring streets and properties.

Phoenix is also moving toward parking maximums in high-transit and urban contexts to prevent over-provision that undermines walkability and land-use efficiency. Parking ratios vary significantly by use type (residential, office, retail, industrial, medical, restaurant, etc.) and by zone or overlay context. All specific ratios below are TBD — verify against the current Phoenix Zoning Ordinance parking chapter. See also [[Parking Ratios by Use]] for cross-jurisdictional context.

## Applicability

The parking standards apply to:
- All new buildings and structures requiring a building permit.
- Changes of occupancy or use that increase parking demand per the ordinance.
- Expansions that exceed defined thresholds (typically when the expansion exceeds a percentage of the existing building area). TBD — verify expansion threshold.
- Exemptions and reductions apply in the TOD overlay, downtown overlay, and for shared parking or in-lieu fee programs. TBD — verify specific exemption conditions.

## Exceptions

- **Shared parking:** Adjacent uses with complementary peak-hour demand may satisfy requirements through a shared parking agreement documented in a recorded reciprocal easement. TBD — verify shared parking eligibility criteria and required documentation.
- **In-lieu fee:** Phoenix may offer an in-lieu fee program in certain areas allowing developers to pay a fee instead of providing required parking. TBD — confirm whether in-lieu program exists, eligible areas, and current fee amounts.
- **Existing non-conforming:** Legal non-conforming parking situations are typically allowed to continue but may not be further reduced. TBD — verify non-conforming continuation rules.
- **Bicycle parking substitution:** A limited number of required vehicle spaces may be replaced with bicycle parking facilities in some zones. TBD — verify substitution ratios and eligibility.
- **TOD/Downtown reductions:** As noted above, the TOD overlay and Downtown Code significantly alter baseline requirements. Consult those overlay notes.
- **Variance:** A variance from parking standards may be available through the [[Phoenix Variance]] process, subject to the standard variance findings.
