# Phoenix Walls and Fences Standards

## Summary

The Phoenix Walls and Fences Standards regulate the height, materials, setback from property lines, and permit requirements for walls, fences, and screening structures throughout the city. The standards serve several purposes: privacy and security for residential properties; visual screening between incompatible land uses (commercial/industrial development adjacent to residential neighborhoods); traffic sight-line safety at intersections; and aesthetic standards consistent with Phoenix's built environment character.

A common and particularly significant requirement in Phoenix is the masonry perimeter wall — commercial and industrial development adjacent to or across an alley from residentially-zoned property is typically required to construct a solid masonry block wall along the shared boundary to buffer noise, light, and visual impacts. This requirement is a major cost and design consideration for commercial infill sites adjacent to existing neighborhoods. ## Applicability

The walls and fences standards apply to:
- All new fences and walls constructed on any property in the city.
- Replacement or modification of existing walls or fences above defined thresholds.
- Required perimeter masonry walls as a condition of commercial, industrial, or multifamily development adjacent to residential zones. TBD — verify adjacency trigger conditions.
- Sound walls or screen walls required as conditions of development approvals.
- Retaining walls (which may have separate structural permit requirements beyond the zoning fence/wall standards). TBD — confirm retaining wall regulatory pathway.

## Exceptions

- **Agricultural/open-use properties:** Properties used for agricultural or large-scale open uses may be subject to different or reduced fencing standards. TBD — verify agricultural fence provisions.
- **Swimming pool barriers:** Fencing required around swimming pools is governed by the Arizona building code pool barrier requirements, which may differ from the general fence height standards and are mandatory for safety.
- **Variance:** A variance from walls and fences standards may be available through the [[Phoenix Variance]] process when physical conditions of the property make standard compliance infeasible.
- **Nonconforming walls:** Existing legally-permitted walls that become nonconforming due to code changes generally may remain but cannot be expanded or substantially reconstructed in their nonconforming configuration.
