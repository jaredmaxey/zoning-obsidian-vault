# Phoenix Lighting Standards

## Summary

The Phoenix Lighting Standards govern outdoor lighting for commercial, industrial, and multifamily development throughout the city. Phoenix sits within the greater Phoenix metro area where outdoor lighting has significant implications for the McDonald Observatory's operations in West Texas and, more locally, for the dark-sky goals of adjacent communities and the preservation of Arizona's legendary clear night skies. Arizona is a national leader in dark-sky protection, and Phoenix's lighting ordinance reflects that broader state and regional commitment.

The standards address three core issues: (1) light trespass — preventing light from a property from spilling onto neighboring properties or public rights-of-way above defined limits; (2) glare — preventing bright, unshielded light sources from creating visibility hazards or discomfort; and (3) sky glow — limiting upward-directed light that contributes to the orange glow visible above the city at night. Practical implications for commercial developers include requirements for full-cutoff luminaires, maximum mounting heights, illuminance limits at property lines, and nighttime curfews or dimming requirements in certain zones. ## Applicability

The lighting standards apply to:
- New commercial, retail, office, and industrial development requiring a building permit.
- New multifamily residential projects.
- Parking lot lighting for new or reconstructed lots.
- Outdoor recreational facilities (sports courts, stadium lighting, fields).
- Canopy lighting (gas stations, drive-throughs).
- Signage illumination (see also [[Phoenix Signage Standards]]).
- Substantial modifications to existing lighting systems above defined replacement thresholds. TBD — verify replacement/modification trigger.

## Exceptions

- **Security lighting:** Certain security lighting applications may qualify for operational flexibility. TBD — verify security lighting provisions and any special standards.
- **Grandfathering:** Existing lighting systems legally installed before the current ordinance may continue as nonconforming but may lose nonconforming status upon significant modification or replacement. TBD — verify nonconforming lighting provisions.
- **Special event permits:** Temporary event lighting (concerts, community events) may be permitted under special event permit provisions with time-limited variances from the lighting standards.
- **Variance:** A variance from lighting standards is available through the [[Phoenix Variance]] process; findings would need to demonstrate hardship while protecting neighboring properties.

Lighting standards apply across all zones. The most demanding compliance situations arise in commercial and industrial zones adjacent to residential uses, and at sites near mountain preserves or open desert where sky glow is most visible.
