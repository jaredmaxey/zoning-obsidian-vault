# Phoenix Landscape Standards

## Summary

The Phoenix Landscape Standards govern the quantity, placement, species selection, and irrigation of landscaping required for new development and substantial redevelopment throughout the city. Given Phoenix's desert climate and extreme heat, the standards emphasize water-efficient, drought-tolerant native and desert-adapted plants over traditional turf or water-intensive species. The standards serve multiple purposes: water conservation, urban heat island mitigation, parking lot shading (to reduce surface temperatures and improve the pedestrian environment), visual buffering between incompatible land uses, and streetscape character.

Phoenix participates in the regional water conservation goals of the Arizona Department of Water Resources and Maricopa Association of Governments. Landscape requirements are therefore closely tied to xeriscape principles — the selection of drought-tolerant plants, efficient drip irrigation, and minimization or elimination of turf in commercial contexts. ## Applicability

The landscape standards apply to:
- New commercial, office, retail, and industrial development projects.
- New multifamily residential projects above a threshold unit count or project size. TBD — verify threshold.
- Substantial expansions or redevelopments (generally when expansion exceeds a defined percentage of the existing structure or parking lot). TBD — verify expansion trigger.
- Parking lots above a threshold number of spaces, where interior parking lot landscaping is required.
- Street frontages on collector and arterial streets where perimeter landscape buffers are required.

## Exceptions

- **Existing development:** Non-conforming landscape situations for existing legal development may continue but must be brought into compliance when a project triggers the landscape requirements through expansion or change of use above defined thresholds. TBD — verify compliance trigger thresholds.
- **Xeriscape alternatives:** Projects demonstrating an equivalent or superior water efficiency outcome through alternative xeriscape designs may be eligible for administrative flexibility in meeting the plant palette or coverage requirements. TBD — confirm flexibility pathway.
- **Urban infill:** Infill projects on constrained urban sites may have reduced landscape requirements or alternative compliance options. TBD — verify infill provisions.
- **Variance:** A variance from landscape standards may be available through the [[Phoenix Variance]] process in cases of physical hardship preventing compliance.
