# Phoenix Site Plan Review

## Summary

Site plan review is the administrative process by which Phoenix reviews the physical design and layout of a proposed development project for compliance with applicable zoning standards, development standards, overlay requirements, and engineering standards before a building permit is issued. For most commercial, industrial, and multifamily development, site plan approval is a required precursor to building permits. The site plan review ensures that the project — as actually designed — conforms to the standards that apply to the parcel, including setbacks, parking, landscaping, access, grading, drainage, and signage.

Site plan review is primarily administrative for projects that are permitted by-right in the applicable zone, meaning that staff applies adopted standards and approves or requests revisions without a public hearing. However, certain project types, larger projects, or projects in overlay districts may require review by the Planning Commission, a Hearing Officer, or a specialized design review body. ## Findings Required

For administrative site plan approvals, there are generally no formal "findings" — instead, staff confirms that the plans demonstrate compliance with all applicable adopted standards. If compliance cannot be demonstrated, staff requests revisions rather than issuing a denial. A formal denial triggers appeal rights.

For projects requiring discretionary review (Planning Commission or Hearing Officer), the decision-maker typically must find:
- The site plan complies with applicable zoning, overlay, and development standards.
- The site is designed to minimize adverse impacts on adjacent properties.
- The project is consistent with the General Plan.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Incomplete submittals:** Missing required plan sheets (especially landscape or photometric plans) delay completeness acceptance and push the review timeline.
- **Not confirming overlay applicability upfront:** Projects in historic, hillside, TOD, or downtown overlay areas have additional standards. Discovering overlay requirements mid-review causes costly redesigns.
- **Parking layout non-compliance:** Drive aisle widths, stall dimensions, and ADA space requirements are common first-review comment items. Review [[Phoenix Parking Standards]] carefully before submitting.
- **Landscape buffer conflicts with site layout:** Perimeter landscape buffers, especially adjacent to residential zones, can significantly constrain building placement and parking configuration.
- **Drainage not addressed:** Stormwater management must be addressed at site plan stage; a drainage concept that won't work may require site redesign.
- **Multiple revision rounds:** Each revision round adds weeks. Front-loading the submittal package with complete and compliant plans is the highest-leverage efficiency strategy.
