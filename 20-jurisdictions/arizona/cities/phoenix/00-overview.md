# Phoenix, Arizona

## Overview

Phoenix is the capital and largest city in Arizona, the most populous city in the American Southwest, and one of the fastest-growing major metros in the United States. Its land area is among the largest of any U.S. city, encompassing diverse terrain from dense urban core to suburban residential tracts, desert preserves, and major employment campuses. The city's zoning regime spans traditional Euclidean base zones, a mature overlay system, and more recently an ambitious Walkable Urban (WU) form-based code framework applied along light-rail corridors and targeted activity centers.

Development in Phoenix is shaped by explosive population growth, significant infill pressure near the urban core and along the Valley Metro light-rail network, and a broader policy shift toward density, mixed use, and walkability. The Planning & Development Department plays a central role in both permit processing and long-range planning. The city's General Plan ("PHX General Plan") establishes the land-use policy framework within which the Zoning Ordinance operates.

## Governing Body

**City Council** — the elected governing body of Phoenix; adopts and amends the Zoning Ordinance, the General Plan, and citywide land-use policy. General Plan amendments, rezonings, and major text amendments require City Council approval.

**Planning Commission** — a seven-member advisory body that hears and makes recommendations to City Council on rezonings, General Plan amendments, and certain large-scale entitlements. The Commission does not have final authority on most land-use matters; it forwards recommendations to Council.

**Zoning Adjustment Hearing Officer (ZAHO)** — an administrative hearing officer who decides variances, certain special permits, and other quasi-judicial matters not requiring full Planning Commission or Council action. The ZAHO provides a streamlined path for relief from dimensional standards.

## Planning Department

The **City of Phoenix Planning & Development Department (PDD)** is the central agency for zoning administration, development review, building permits, and long-range planning. It administers the Zoning Ordinance, processes site plan reviews, conditional use permits, rezoning applications, and General Plan amendments.

Primary URL: https://www.phoenix.gov/pdd

## General Plan / Comprehensive Plan

Phoenix operates under the **PHX General Plan**, the city's long-range policy document guiding land use, transportation, housing, and economic development. Arizona state law (ARS Title 9) requires Arizona municipalities to adopt and periodically update a General Plan. The PHX General Plan establishes land-use designations that must be consistent with Zoning Ordinance classifications.

## Subdivision Standards

Large subdivisions typically require a Tentative Development Plan (TDP) and Final Plat approval through PDD and City Council.

## Permit Timelines

Phoenix PDD processes a range of permit and entitlement types at different timescales:

- **Administrative/By-Right permits** (building permits for conforming uses): typically weeks to a few months depending on project complexity and current queue volume.
- **Site Plan Review**: varies by project size; PDD offers concurrent review for larger projects.
- **Conditional Use Permit (CUP)**: requires a public hearing before the Zoning Adjustment Hearing Officer or Planning Commission; typically a multi-month process.
- **Rezoning / General Plan Amendment**: City Council action required; major rezonings with environmental review may take 6–18+ months.
- **Planned Development (PD)**: negotiated; timelines vary based on complexity and negotiation with staff.
