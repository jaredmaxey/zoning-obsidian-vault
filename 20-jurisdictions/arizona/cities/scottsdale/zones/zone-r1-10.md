# Scottsdale R1-10

## Summary

The R1-10 district requires a minimum lot size of approximately 10,000 square feet (roughly 0.23 acres), producing a conventional suburban single-family character with densities in the approximate range of 3–4 dwelling units per acre. This is a common zone designation for post-war and 1960s–1980s-era Scottsdale neighborhoods in central and south Scottsdale, where a standard suburban-block pattern of detached single-family homes on modest but adequately sized lots is the built form.

R1-10 is widely applied across the older, more established sections of Scottsdale and forms the backbone of many mid-city residential communities. It is a frequent base zone in tract-development contexts.
