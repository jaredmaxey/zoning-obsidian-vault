# Scottsdale PNC

## Summary

The PNC (Planned Neighborhood Center) district accommodates neighborhood shopping centers — typically anchored by a grocery store or supermarket with in-line retail, personal services, restaurants, and financial services serving a defined neighborhood trade area. The PNC is larger in scale than the PCoC and is positioned to serve the primary weekly shopping needs of surrounding residential communities.

In Scottsdale, PNC centers are a common element of master-planned communities and residential corridors, typically sited at major arterial intersections. Each PNC is developed as a planned center with a site plan establishing the center's layout, access, and use mix.

Planned Neighborhood Center district — all dimensional standards are established per the individual approved PNC development plan/stipulations. No uniform numeric standards apply; review the specific approved plan for each property.
