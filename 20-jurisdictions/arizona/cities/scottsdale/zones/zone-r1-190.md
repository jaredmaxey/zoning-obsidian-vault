# Scottsdale R1-190

## Summary

The R1-190 district is Scottsdale's largest base single-family residential zone, establishing a minimum lot size of approximately 190,000 square feet (roughly 4.4 acres). It is applied to the most rural and environmentally sensitive fringe areas of the city — primarily large-lot estate and ranch parcels in the far-north Scottsdale desert corridor, where preservation of open space, viewsheds, and sensitive desert habitat is a primary planning objective. The very large minimum lot size effectively limits density to well under one dwelling unit per acre, producing a semi-rural character consistent with surrounding Maricopa County Desert Rural and State Trust lands.

Development in the R1-190 district typically occurs as custom single-family homes on large acreage parcels. The district interacts heavily with the Scottsdale Environmentally Sensitive Lands Overlay (ESLO) and Foothills Overlay, which may impose additional site analysis, hillside protection, and grading limits on top of the base-zone standards.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by citywide subdivision standards — see [[Scottsdale Subdivision Platting]]. Large-lot parcel splits may be subject to simplified plat or lot-split procedures; verify with Planning and Development Services.

## Overlay Interactions

The [[Scottsdale Environmentally Sensitive Lands Overlay]] (ESLO) frequently applies to R1-190 parcels in hillside and desert-wash areas, imposing supplemental site analysis, viewshed protection, and grading restrictions. The Foothills Overlay may also apply in the McDowell Mountain foothills area. Both overlays layer on top of base-zone standards and may be the binding constraint on development.
