# Scottsdale D

## Summary

The D (Downtown) district is Scottsdale's urban mixed-use zone for the Old Town/Downtown core, designed to support the highest-density and most diverse mix of uses within the city. It accommodates retail, restaurant, bar and entertainment, office, hotel, multi-family residential (typically upper-floor), civic, and cultural uses in a compact, walkable urban form. The Downtown district is intended to reinforce Old Town Scottsdale's identity as a regional entertainment, arts, and hospitality destination while also encouraging residential density and office development that support the area's vitality.

The D district typically operates in conjunction with the [[Scottsdale Downtown (Old Town) Overlay]] (DO), which layered on top of the D base zone adds further design standards, use-specific regulations, and area-specific development requirements. Together, the D district and the Downtown Overlay form the regulatory framework for Scottsdale's most complex and high-value development context.

Standards transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) §5.3006 (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative. The Downtown district has multiple development type sub-areas (Types 1/2/2.5/3) each with distinct height limits; confirm applicable type for specific parcels against the §5.3006.C table.

## Overlay Interactions

The [[Scottsdale Downtown (Old Town) Overlay]] (DO) applies across most of the D district and is the primary regulatory instrument for design review, use specifics, and intensity in the Downtown core. Nearly all development in the D district requires compliance with the DO. The P-3 Parking District may also apply, allowing shared/reduced parking in the Downtown core.
