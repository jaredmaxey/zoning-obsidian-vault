# Scottsdale P-C

## Summary

The P-C (Planned Community) district is Scottsdale's master-planned community zone, enabling large-scale comprehensive developments that include multiple land-use types — residential, commercial, office, civic, and open space — within a unified development plan approved by the City Council. Each P-C approval results in a site-specific development plan that establishes the permitted uses, density, development standards, and phasing specific to that project, superseding the default standards of the underlying base zoning.

The P-C framework was developed to accommodate major master-planned community developments — the large, mixed-use communities (often hundreds to thousands of acres) that defined Scottsdale's growth in the 1980s through 2000s. Each P-C is a negotiated entitlement unique to its project. Development within an approved P-C follows the approved development plan rather than the general ordinance provisions.

Planned Community district — all dimensional standards are established per the individual approved P-C development plan/stipulations. No uniform numeric standards apply; review the specific approved P-C plan for each property. Framework provisions transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by the P-C development plan and citywide subdivision standards — see [[Scottsdale Subdivision Platting]]. Large P-C communities typically include comprehensive subdivision and phasing provisions in the development plan.

## Overlay Interactions

Overlay districts may still apply within a P-C depending on location — e.g., [[Scottsdale Environmentally Sensitive Lands Overlay]] in desert areas. Verify whether specific overlays apply for each P-C parcel.

## Process & Approvals Required

- P-C designation / amendment: [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] (City Council action required)
- Within an approved P-C, individual parcel/phase review: [[Site Plan Review]]
- Modifications to approved P-C plan: [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] or administrative amendment
- Variance from P-C plan standards: [[Variance]]
- General development review: [[Scottsdale Development Review]]
