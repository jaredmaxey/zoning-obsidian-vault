# Scottsdale PCoC

## Summary

The PCoC (Planned Convenience Center) district is the smallest of Scottsdale's planned commercial center zones, providing for small convenience-commercial centers that serve the immediate neighborhood trade area. Typical uses include convenience stores, quick-service food, personal services, and small-format retail serving immediate resident populations. The PCoC is designed to be the least intensive planned center format and is applied at neighborhood corners and small commercial nodes.

The "planned" designation indicates that each PCoC development requires a site plan that establishes the center's configuration, access, landscaping, and use mix as an integrated planned development rather than a standard commercial lot.

Standards transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) §5.2705 (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative. Note: PCoC (§5.2700–§5.2709) IS present in the official Appendix B with a complete PDS section — contrary to any earlier assumption that it was absent from the ordinance.
