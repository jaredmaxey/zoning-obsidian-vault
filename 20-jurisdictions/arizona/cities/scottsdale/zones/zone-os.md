# Scottsdale OS

## Summary

The OS (Open Space) district is Scottsdale's base zone designation for publicly accessible open space, parks, preserves, and recreation lands. It governs land designated for passive and active recreation, nature preserves, desert preserve areas, golf courses, and similar open-space functions. The OS designation is applied to city parks, the McDowell Sonoran Preserve (one of the largest urban preserves in the country), public trail systems, and other lands where preservation and open-space use is the primary function.

The OS district has very limited permitted building intensity and is designed to maintain lands in a predominantly undeveloped or minimally developed state. Development of infrastructure serving the open space (restrooms, parking areas, trailheads, maintenance facilities) may be permitted subject to site plan review.
