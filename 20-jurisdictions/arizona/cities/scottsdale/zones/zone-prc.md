# Scottsdale PRC

## Summary

The PRC (Planned Regional Center) district is Scottsdale's large-scale planned commercial zone for regional shopping centers and major mixed-use commercial nodes that serve a broad regional trade area. It accommodates full-size department store anchors, large-format retail, cinemas, hotels, restaurants, entertainment, and office uses in a planned center format. The PRC is the planned-center equivalent of the C-S (Regional Shopping Center) base zone, adding a development-plan component that governs the center's specific layout, phasing, and use mix.

PRC centers are typically large parcels (50+ acres) at major freeway interchanges and primary arterial intersections serving the regional market. The planned nature of the designation requires City Council approval and a comprehensive site and use plan.

Planned Regional Center district — all dimensional standards are established per the individual approved PRC development plan/stipulations. No uniform numeric standards apply; review the specific approved plan for each property. Framework provisions transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative.
