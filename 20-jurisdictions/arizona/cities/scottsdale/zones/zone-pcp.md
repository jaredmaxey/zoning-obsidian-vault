# Scottsdale PCP

## Summary

The PCP (Planned Commerce Park) district is Scottsdale's planned employment-zone framework for the Scottsdale Airpark and similar large-scale business-park development areas. It accommodates office, research and development, flex-industrial, and business-park uses within a planned, campus-quality development context. The PCP is a planned-center analog for the industrial/employment sector — akin to the PRC for retail but applied to business/industrial parks.

The Scottsdale Airpark is the dominant PCP context in the city: a major employment center along the Loop 101 corridor hosting hundreds of businesses across office, flex-industrial, R&D, and aviation-support uses. Each PCP development requires an approved commerce park plan that establishes the permitted uses, layout, access, and development standards for the park.

Planned Commerce Park district — all dimensional standards are established per the individual approved PCP development plan/stipulations. No uniform numeric standards apply; review the specific approved plan for each property. Framework provisions transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative.
