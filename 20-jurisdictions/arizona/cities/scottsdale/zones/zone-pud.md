# Scottsdale PUD

## Summary

The PUD (Planned Unit Development) district is Scottsdale's flexible planned-development zoning mechanism, enabling applicants to propose custom use programs, development standards, and site designs that depart from standard base-zone regulations in exchange for demonstrably superior design outcomes, public benefits, or community objectives. Unlike the large master-planned P-C district, the PUD is typically applied to smaller parcels or assemblages where a creative or mixed-use development concept requires regulatory flexibility.

PUDs are commonly used in Scottsdale for mixed-use residential and commercial projects, high-density infill residential, adaptive-reuse developments, and projects in transitional locations where a standard base zone would be overly constraining. Each PUD is a negotiated entitlement requiring City Council approval with a project-specific development plan that governs permitted uses and development standards.

Planned Unit Development district — all dimensional standards are established per the individual approved PUD development plan/stipulations. No uniform numeric standards apply; review the specific approved PUD plan for each property.
