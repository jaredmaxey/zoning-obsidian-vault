# Scottsdale R1-5

## Summary

The R1-5 district is Scottsdale's smallest single-family residential zone. Notably, despite the "5" in its name, the actual minimum lot size is approximately 4,700 square feet per lot as stated in the ordinance — not 5,000 square feet — a distinction that should be verified against the current code text. At this lot size, the zone can support densities approaching 8–9 dwelling units per acre, representing the most urban scale of detached single-family development in Scottsdale.

R1-5 is applied in dense infill locations, particularly in south Scottsdale and urban transitional areas where small-lot single-family and cottage-style development is the target built form. It is frequently found adjacent to multi-family and commercial zones and serves as a transitional buffer in residential-to-commercial corridors.
