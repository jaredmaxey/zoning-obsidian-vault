# Scottsdale R1-43

## Summary

The R1-43 district requires a minimum lot size of approximately 43,000 square feet (roughly 1 acre), placing it in the "one-acre lot" tier of Scottsdale single-family zoning. This district is prevalent in mid-north Scottsdale communities developed in the 1980s and 1990s where one-acre horse properties and custom-home subdivisions were a primary land-use pattern. The one-acre minimum supports both residential custom-home development and limited equestrian uses typical of that era.

The R1-43 district may still interact with the ESLO in certain locations but is less likely than the larger R1 tiers to be fully overlay-constrained. It is a commonly encountered zone in Scottsdale's established mid-luxury residential corridors.

Standards transcribed from the official Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (Appendix B) §5.104 (provided 2026-05-25); spot-check recommended before promoting to authoritative.
