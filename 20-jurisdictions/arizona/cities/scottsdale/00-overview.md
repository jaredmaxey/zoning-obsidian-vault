# Scottsdale, Arizona

## Overview

Scottsdale is a high-growth, high-wealth city in the eastern portion of Maricopa County, Arizona, known for luxury residential development, a robust resort and hospitality sector, an active Old Town/Downtown commercial core, and significant Class A office and medical campuses. The city spans a large geographic area extending north into the McDowell Mountain foothills and the Sonoran Desert Preserve, which makes environmental and hillside regulation a distinctive feature of its development framework. Scottsdale's planning posture balances economic development in select nodes (Old Town, the Health-care District, the Airpark/Loop 101 corridor) against strong community expectations for design quality, open space preservation, and neighborhood character protection.

Scottsdale administers its own comprehensive zoning ordinance and general plan, operating as an Arizona home-rule municipality under Article XIII of the Arizona Constitution and the enabling authority of ARS Title 9 (municipalities). Development review in Scottsdale is notable for layered design standards — applicants routinely face both base-zone standards and overlay or area-specific plan requirements simultaneously, making pre-application coordination with Planning and Development Services essential.

## Governing Body

**City Council** is the ultimate legislative authority over zoning, including general plan amendments, rezonings, and code text amendments. Scottsdale uses a council-manager form of government; the Mayor and six Council members are elected at-large.

**Planning Commission** is a seven-member advisory body appointed by City Council. The Commission holds public hearings on general plan amendments, rezonings, and major conditional use permits, and forwards recommendations to Council. For certain process types the Commission serves as the final decision-maker — verify the specific delegation against the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance (needs-verification).

**Board of Adjustment** hears variance requests and appeals of administrative interpretations. The Board's decisions are quasi-judicial and may be appealed to Superior Court.

## Planning Department

**Planning and Development Services Department** administers zoning, land use, building permits, inspections, and development review. The department operates a Development Services Center as the primary point of contact for project submittals. URL: https://www.scottsdaleaz.gov/planning

## General Plan / Comprehensive Plan

Scottsdale's **General Plan** (referred to locally as the General Plan) is the long-range land-use and policy framework guiding city development. The plan establishes Future Land Use designations that control rezonings and entitlements. Arizona law (ARS § 9-461 et seq.) requires municipalities of Scottsdale's size to adopt and periodically update a general plan; the current plan horizon and last update year should be verified against https://www.scottsdaleaz.gov/planning/general-plan. (needs-verification)

The General Plan's Future Land Use Map and character-area policies are a critical threshold test for any rezoning or general plan amendment — consistency with the GP designation is typically a prerequisite, or else a concurrent GPA is required.
