# Scottsdale Development Review

## Summary

Development review in Scottsdale encompasses the administrative review of site plans, building elevations, landscape plans, and related project documents to confirm compliance with base zoning standards, citywide development standards, and any applicable overlay or area-specific plan requirements. For projects meeting certain size, use, or location thresholds, Scottsdale routes applications through a **Design Review Board** (or equivalent body) that evaluates architectural quality, site design, and compatibility — a feature distinguishing Scottsdale from many peer cities. Understanding whether a project triggers administrative review only versus formal design review is essential for timeline planning. See [[Site Plan Review]] for the general concept.

## Triggering Conditions

Site plan review is required for most new construction, major additions, and changes of use above minimum thresholds. Design review (the elevated track) is typically triggered by: commercial or multi-family projects above a certain square footage or unit count; projects within design-sensitive overlays such as the Downtown/Old Town area; developments adjacent to residential zones or open space; or projects flagged in a rezoning condition of approval. The exact triggers must be confirmed against the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance. (needs-verification)

Minor remodels, interior work, and small additions may qualify for streamlined or counter review.

## Decision-Maker

Administrative site plan review is handled by **Planning and Development Services** staff. Projects meeting design review thresholds go to a **Design Review Board** (or Architectural Review Panel — verify current body name against the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance). (needs-verification)

Staff review is an administrative ministerial action — compliance with standards leads to approval. Design review introduces discretionary evaluation of design quality, which can result in conditions requiring design modifications.

## Findings Required

Administrative site plan review is a compliance determination — the standard is whether the plans conform to all applicable ordinance requirements. There is no discretion to approve a nonconforming plan; the applicant must either revise or pursue a variance/waiver.

For discretionary design review, findings typically center on compatibility with surrounding context, design quality relative to the applicable design guidelines, and compliance with any overlay standards. ## Common Pitfalls

- **Underestimating design review:** Scottsdale's design review process can impose material design changes after significant investment in construction documents — engage the process early and study comparable approvals in the area.
- **Overlay requirements overlooked:** Projects in the Downtown/Old Town area or other overlays carry additional standards on top of the base zone; check all applicable layers before finalizing the design.
- **Landscape and lighting plan quality:** Scottsdale has robust landscape and lighting standards; submitting underdeveloped plans triggers multiple resubmittal cycles.
- **Stale submittals:** If a project goes through multiple design iterations over many months, ensure the site plan reflects current ordinance requirements — code amendments between submittals can create new compliance issues.
