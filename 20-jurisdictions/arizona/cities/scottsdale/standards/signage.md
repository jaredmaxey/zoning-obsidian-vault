# Scottsdale Signage Standards

## Summary

Scottsdale's sign regulations govern the type, size, height, placement, and illumination of all on-premises signage for commercial, industrial, and multi-family development. The regulations reflect the city's design-conscious character: Scottsdale generally emphasizes monument signs over tall pole signs, limits digital and changeable-copy signs, and imposes stricter standards in design-sensitive areas such as the Downtown/Old Town overlay. Signage compliance is reviewed as part of the development review process, and a separate sign permit is required for most new sign installations. Sign regulations must be read alongside any applicable overlay or area-specific plan requirements, which can be more restrictive than base-zone standards.

## Applicability

Scottsdale's sign standards apply to all new signage installations, modifications to existing signs, and changes of sign copy or structure. Exempt signs (typically small directional, temporary, and governmental signs below a minimum size) are enumerated in the ordinance — verify the current exemption list. The Downtown/Old Town overlay and other special area plans impose additional or different standards that supersede the base sign code in their geographic area. (needs-verification)

## Exceptions

- **Variance:** Sign area or height variances are available through the Board of Adjustment under the standard variance findings — economic hardship alone is not sufficient.
- **Administrative adjustment:** Minor deviations may be addressable through an administrative process — verify thresholds against the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance. (needs-verification)
- **Master sign programs:** Multi-tenant developments commonly obtain a Master Sign Program approval establishing a coordinated signage scheme, which may include modifications to standard requirements in exchange for design coordination — verify process with Planning and Development Services.
