# Scottsdale Parking Standards

## Summary

Scottsdale's parking standards establish minimum off-street parking requirements for all land uses, dimensional standards for stalls and drive aisles, bicycle parking requirements, and the mechanisms available to reduce or share parking. The standards apply citywide and are supplemented in certain overlays — most notably the Downtown/Old Town overlay area where reduced parking or parking structure approaches may be encouraged or required. Parking requirements for uses in areas subject to the Environmentally Sensitive Lands Ordinance or other special overlays may carry additional design constraints. See [[Parking Ratios by Use]] for a cross-jurisdictional comparison table.

## Applicability

Scottsdale's parking standards generally apply to all new construction, additions that increase the parking demand of a use, and changes of use that trigger additional required parking. Existing legally nonconforming parking situations may be grandfathered in certain circumstances — verify the nonconformity provisions against the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance. (needs-verification)

## Exceptions

- **Shared parking agreements:** When adjacent uses have complementary peak-hour demand profiles, a shared parking study may support a reduction. The shared parking methodology and required documentation should be verified.
- **Downtown/Old Town area reductions:** The Old Town overlay or specific area plan may allow reduced parking ratios or contributions to a parking-in-lieu program — verify.
- **Transit-oriented reductions:** Proximity to transit may support reductions in certain contexts — verify whether Scottsdale's ordinance provides this.
- **Bicycle parking substitution:** Some parking spaces may be convertible to bicycle parking facilities — verify.
- **Administrative adjustment or variance:** Minor deviations from parking requirements may be obtainable through administrative processes or the Board of Adjustment — verify thresholds.
