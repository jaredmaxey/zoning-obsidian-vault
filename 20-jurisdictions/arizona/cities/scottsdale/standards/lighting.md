# Scottsdale Lighting Standards

## Summary

Scottsdale's outdoor lighting standards are shaped by the city's commitment to dark-sky preservation, compatibility with its desert and hillside environments, and the protection of residential areas from light trespass and glare from adjacent commercial or industrial development. The standards generally require full-cutoff or fully-shielded fixtures that direct light downward, set maximum illuminance levels for parking lots and other exterior areas, restrict light trespass at property lines, and impose additional restrictions in environmentally sensitive and hillside areas. A photometric plan is required as part of the development review submittal package. Scottsdale's lighting standards are more stringent than many peer cities, particularly in areas near the McDowell Sonoran Preserve.

## Applicability

Scottsdale's lighting standards apply to all new exterior lighting for commercial, industrial, multi-family, and institutional uses, and to remodels that materially alter site lighting. The Environmentally Sensitive Lands Overlay (ESLO) imposes additional restrictions on lighting in hillside and preserve-adjacent areas. (needs-verification)
