# Scottsdale Landscape Standards

## Summary

Scottsdale's landscape standards reflect the city's desert identity and commitment to water-efficient, low-maintenance, visually high-quality landscaping across all development types. The standards address minimum landscape area percentages, required plant densities and species selections (emphasizing Sonoran Desert-adapted and drought-tolerant material), parking lot tree shading requirements, perimeter and buffer yard widths between incompatible uses, and water conservation through efficient irrigation. Scottsdale's landscape standards are among the more design-focused in the Phoenix metro and are actively reviewed during the development review and design review processes. Applicants routinely find that under-developed landscape plans are the most common cause of resubmittal cycles.

## Applicability

Scottsdale's landscape requirements apply to new commercial, industrial, multi-family, and institutional development, and to substantial remodels or additions above established thresholds. Single-family residential landscape requirements may operate under a different framework — verify. Special requirements apply in the Environmentally Sensitive Lands overlay (see [[Scottsdale Environmentally Sensitive Lands Overlay]]), where native plant preservation and re-use of salvaged desert vegetation may be required.
