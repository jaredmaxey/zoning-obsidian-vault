# Scottsdale Walls and Fences Standards

## Summary

Scottsdale's walls and fences standards regulate the height, placement, materials, and design of perimeter walls, screening walls, retaining walls, and fencing for residential, commercial, and industrial development. The standards serve multiple purposes: screening mechanical equipment and service areas from public view and adjacent residential uses, providing security, establishing visual continuity along street frontages, and managing grade transitions on hillside sites. In keeping with Scottsdale's design character, wall materials and finishes are subject to design review scrutiny — plain block walls visible from public rights-of-way are generally discouraged in design-sensitive areas. Additional restrictions apply in hillside and ESLO areas where walls must be designed to minimize visual impact on the desert landscape.

## Applicability

Walls and fences standards apply to new construction and to modifications to existing walls that increase height or change location. Commercial screening wall requirements are triggered by adjacency to residential zones and by the need to screen outdoor storage, mechanical equipment, trash enclosures, and loading areas. Retaining walls on sloped sites in ESLO areas may carry additional design requirements. (needs-verification)

## Exceptions

- **Variance:** Deviations from maximum heights require Board of Adjustment approval; screening wall height deviations may alternatively be addressed administratively — verify thresholds. (needs-verification)
- **Alternative screening:** Landscaping or berming may be accepted in lieu of a wall in certain contexts — verify the alternative screening provisions in the Scottsdale Zoning Ordinance.
