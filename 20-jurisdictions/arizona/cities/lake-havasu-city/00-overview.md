# Lake Havasu City, Arizona

## Overview

Lake Havasu City is a Mohave County city on the eastern shore of Lake Havasu, a reservoir on the Colorado River along the Arizona-California border. The city is internationally recognized for the relocated London Bridge — a heritage and tourism attraction that anchors a waterfront commercial district and draws substantial visitor traffic year-round, with peaks during spring break and summer boating season.

The city's economic base is built on tourism, recreation, retirement, and second-home demand. The Colorado River waterfront and mild winter climate have made it a premier Arizona destination for boaters, outdoor recreationalists, and snowbirds. A regional hospital and healthcare sector serve the broader Mohave County population.

For CRE, Lake Havasu City's market is driven by hospitality (hotels, vacation rentals, boat-storage facilities), waterfront-adjacent retail and restaurant, and residential development serving retirees and second-home buyers. Industrial is limited. The city is substantially built out in its core areas, with some peripheral residential development continuing. A [[Highest and Best Use Analysis]] for waterfront or near-waterfront parcels should price in the tourism premium carefully.

## Governing Body

Lake Havasu City operates under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings and general plan amendments.
