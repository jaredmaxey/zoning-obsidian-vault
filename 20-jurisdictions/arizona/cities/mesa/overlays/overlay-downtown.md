# Mesa Downtown/Town Center Overlay

## Summary

Mesa's Downtown and/or Town Center overlay district applies supplemental development standards and use regulations to the downtown core — the historic center of Mesa bounded roughly by Main Street, the light-rail alignment, and surrounding blocks (`TBD — verify exact boundaries against the official overlay map` `needs-verification`). The overlay's primary purpose is to encourage higher-intensity mixed-use development, an activated pedestrian realm, and urban building forms that depart from the suburban setback-and-parking-lot paradigm typical of base commercial zones. As the Valley Metro Rail system runs through downtown Mesa, the overlay intersects with the city's transit-oriented development strategy.

The overlay typically modifies base zone standards in areas such as building setbacks (reducing or eliminating front setbacks to encourage street-fronting buildings), parking minimums (reducing or waiving minimums near transit), ground-floor use requirements (active/retail uses at street level), building height opportunities (potentially allowing greater height than the underlying zone), and design review requirements for façade treatment and street activation.
