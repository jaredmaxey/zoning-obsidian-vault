# Mesa Transit Overlay District

## Summary

The Mesa Transit Overlay District (name `TBD — verify official name in the Mesa Zoning Ordinance` `needs-verification`) is a zoning overlay applied within defined walking-distance radii of Valley Metro Rail light-rail stations in Mesa. The Valley Metro Rail system extended into Mesa along Main Street, connecting to the East Mesa extension to Gilbert Road (`TBD — verify current station locations and any planned extensions` `needs-verification`). The transit overlay responds to these investments by creating a regulatory framework that enables — and in some cases requires — higher-density, mixed-use, pedestrian-oriented development in station areas.

The transit overlay is one of the most CRE-relevant overlays in Mesa for infill and mixed-use development projects. It typically unlocks higher residential densities, allows mixed use by right or with reduced barriers, reduces or eliminates parking minimums that would otherwise burden transit-adjacent sites, and imposes design standards requiring pedestrian activation, bicycle infrastructure, and building placement that addresses the street rather than the parking lot.

## Geographic Extent

Applies within a defined radius around each Valley Metro Rail station in Mesa (often a quarter-mile or half-mile walking shed, with different standards tiers at different distances from the platform). ## Additional Requirements

Projects in the transit overlay may be required to provide enhanced pedestrian connections to the nearest light-rail station, bicycle storage facilities, and street-level design consistent with an active, human-scale environment.
