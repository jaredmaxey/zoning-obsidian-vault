# Mesa Overlay Districts (Index)

## Summary

Mesa employs overlay districts and specific area plans to apply supplemental or modified development standards and use regulations within defined geographic areas — typically priority redevelopment zones, transit corridors, and areas where standard base-zone regulations are insufficient to achieve desired development character. Overlays sit on top of a base zone district; a parcel subject to an overlay must comply with both the base zone standards and the overlay standards (with the overlay typically controlling where standards conflict).

## How Overlays Are Applied

A parcel is subject to an overlay if it falls within the overlay's geographic boundary as shown on the official zoning/overlay map. Parcel-specific overlay status should be confirmed through the Mesa GIS portal or directly with Development Services. In cases of conflict between base zone standards and overlay standards, the overlay typically governs, but this should be confirmed for each specific overlay provision.
