# Mesa DC

## Summary

The Downtown Core (DC) District is the apex of Mesa's zoning intensity hierarchy, governing development at the heart of the city's urban core. DC permits the broadest range of uses at the highest densities — vertical mixed-use towers, full-service hotels, entertainment venues, civic and cultural institutions, high-density residential, and ground-floor active uses are all contemplated. DC reflects Mesa's long-term vision for a vibrant, dense, transit-connected downtown serving as the city's economic and cultural center, activated by Valley Metro Rail.

The Form-Based Code (Article 6, Chapters 56-64 of Mesa City Code Title 11) is an opt-in alternative regulatory framework applicable in the downtown area including DC-zoned parcels, allowing predictable high-quality building form outcomes without discretionary review when standards are met. The Form-Based Code was substantially updated by a 2025 amendment (Planning & Zoning Board recommendation May 28, 2025).

## Overlay Interactions

Form-Based Code (Art. 6, Chs. 56-64) is opt-in alternative — covers downtown and Temple/Pioneer Park neighborhoods. Updated via 2025 Form-Based Code Amendments. Transit overlay near Valley Metro Rail stations. Historic overlay (Ch. 74) may apply to historic buildings within DC area.
