# Mesa RM-5

## Summary

The RM-5 (Residential Multiple Dwelling) District is Mesa's highest-density base residential zone, supporting mid-rise and high-rise apartment development at urban densities. RM-5 is used along major arterials, near downtown, and in transit-oriented development corridors where the city seeks to concentrate residential density. It is the apex of Mesa's residential density series and often occurs in conjunction with MX or GC zones in mixed-use development areas.

Ordinance ZON17-00606 (April 2020) applied RM-5 as part of the Hawes Crossing PAD for multifamily components in a large master-planned community. Open space standards for RM-5 are governed by §11-5-5 and §11-6-5 of the Mesa Zoning Ordinance. ADUs are permitted per Ordinance 5883 (Oct 2024).
