# Mesa AG

## Summary

The Agricultural (AG) District is Mesa's lowest-intensity base zone, designed to preserve large-parcel rural and agricultural land uses at the city's urban fringe and in areas transitioning from rural to developed character. It accommodates working farms, ranches, equestrian uses, and rural single-family residential development on substantial acreage. The AG district is commonly found in the far eastern and southern portions of Mesa's planning area, including areas near Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport and the rural edge of the Superstition Mountains foothills planning area.

The district's large minimum lot sizes and rural character make it a holding zone for land that has not yet been committed to urban development patterns. Rezoning out of AG is a common entitlement step for large-scale master-planned communities entering Mesa's development pipeline. ## Overlay Interactions

The PAD (Planned Area Development) overlay is commonly applied when AG land is rezoned for master-planned development. Airport compatibility overlays may apply near Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport per Mesa ZO §11-19.
