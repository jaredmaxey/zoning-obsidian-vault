# Mesa DR-1

## Summary

The Downtown Residence District 1 (DR-1) is the lowest-density tier of Mesa's three Downtown Residence districts, governing residential development within and adjacent to the downtown planning area. DR-1 accommodates traditional neighborhood housing types — single-family and low-density multifamily — in a context that acknowledges the proximity to downtown amenities and urban services. Development in DR-1 reflects the established residential character of neighborhoods adjacent to the Mesa downtown core.

ADUs are permitted in DR districts per Ordinance 5883 (Oct 2024) where a single-family residence exists. Downtown Residence districts are governed by Mesa ZO Chapter 8 (Downtown Districts).

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by citywide Mesa subdivision standards. Historic district provisions (Ch. 74) may apply in portions of the downtown residential area.

## Overlay Interactions

Historic overlay (Ch. 74) may apply. Transit overlay near Valley Metro Rail stations. Form-Based Code (Art. 6, Chs. 56-60) is an optional overlay for downtown properties.
