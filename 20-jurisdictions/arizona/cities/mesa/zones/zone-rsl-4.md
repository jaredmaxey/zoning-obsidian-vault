# Mesa RSL-4

## Summary

The RSL-4 (Residential Small Lot Single Dwelling) District is the largest of Mesa's small-lot single-family zones, with a nominally ~4,000 sf minimum lot size (verify exact standard; note the ordinance uses "RSL-4.0" in some documents — treat RSL-4 and RSL-4.0 as the same district). RSL-4 bridges the gap between the compact RSL-2.5/RSL-3 zones and conventional RS-6 single-family development, supporting detached and attached single-family housing with alley-loaded options. It is commonly applied in new master-planned communities.

Ordinance ZON17-00606 (April 2020) applied RSL-4 (referred to as RSL-4.0) alongside RSL-2.5, RS-6, and RM-5 in the Hawes Crossing PAD, with specific design requirements: alley-loaded product, shared driveways, mixed lot widths, covered entries, and front porches.
