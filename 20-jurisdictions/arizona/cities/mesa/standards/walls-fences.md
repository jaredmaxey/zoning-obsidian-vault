# Mesa Walls and Fences Standards

## Summary

Mesa's walls and fences standards establish maximum heights, material requirements, and placement rules for perimeter walls, screen walls, and decorative fences across different zoning contexts. In the Southwest development pattern, masonry perimeter walls are standard at commercial-to-residential interfaces, providing visual screening, noise attenuation, and security. Mesa's standards address both the height of walls in various locations on a lot (front yard, side yard, rear yard, corner lots) and the required bufferwalls between incompatible uses. Standards vary by zone district and whether the wall is in a required setback or bufferyard.

Permit requirements apply for walls above a certain height threshold. (`TBD — verify current permit threshold for walls and fences in Mesa` `needs-verification`) Screen walls enclosing refuse enclosures, mechanical equipment, and loading areas have their own standards and are typically required as part of site plan approval.

## Applicability

Applies to commercial, industrial, and multifamily development for perimeter and screening walls. Single-family residential fences are governed by residential fence standards. Required bufferwalls between commercial/industrial and adjacent residential zones are a condition of development approval. (`TBD — verify specific applicability and triggers` `needs-verification`)

## Exceptions

Variances from wall height or placement standards are available through the Board of Adjustment (see [[Mesa Variance]]). Decorative wall designs that vary from standard materials may be approved as part of an overall site plan or master plan.
