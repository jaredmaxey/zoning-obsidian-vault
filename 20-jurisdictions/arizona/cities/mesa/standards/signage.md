# Mesa Signage Standards

## Summary

Mesa's sign regulations govern the type, size, height, placement, and illumination of signs for commercial, industrial, and multifamily uses. The standards are intended to balance effective business identification with visual quality along corridors and in residential-adjacent areas. Sign permits are required for most permanent signs; a sign plan or master sign program may be required for multi-tenant developments and planned commercial centers. The sign standards reflect federal and state law constraints on content-based sign regulation following Reed v. Town of Gilbert.

Overlay districts — particularly the Downtown/Town Center overlay and specific area plans for major commercial corridors — may impose supplemental or modified sign standards. Applicants proposing projects in these areas should review overlay-specific sign provisions in addition to the base ordinance.

## Applicability

Applies to all permanent and certain temporary signs on commercial, industrial, mixed-use, and multifamily properties. Residential single-family signage is subject to more limited provisions. Special rules apply to freeway-oriented signs, electronic message boards, and billboards (off-premises signs). (`TBD — verify specific applicability and sign type categories` `needs-verification`)
