# Mesa Parking Standards

## Summary

Mesa's off-street parking standards establish minimum required parking ratios by use type, dimensional requirements for stalls and aisles, and design standards for parking lots. These requirements apply citywide to all new development and significant expansions. Within certain overlay areas — particularly the downtown/town center overlay and transit-oriented development zones near light-rail stations — reduced parking minimums or parking maximums may apply to encourage transit use and pedestrian-oriented development. See [[Parking Ratios by Use]] for cross-jurisdictional comparison.

Parking design standards govern stall dimensions, aisle widths, drive approach placement, wheel stops, striping, handicap-accessible spaces (per ADA and state law), and landscaping within surface parking lots (cross-referenced with the landscape ordinance). Bicycle parking requirements may also apply for certain use types.

## Applicability

Applies to all new construction, changes of use, and expansions of existing buildings that trigger parking standard compliance. Surface and structured parking, as well as shared parking arrangements, are governed. Exemptions and reductions may be available for historic buildings, transit-adjacent sites, shared parking agreements, or reduced-demand uses. ## Exceptions

Reductions to required parking may be available through:

- Shared parking agreements between compatible uses with offset peak demand periods
- Proximity to transit (Valley Metro Rail stations) — reduced minimum or waiver in TOD overlay areas (`TBD — verify reduction amounts` `needs-verification`)
- Participation in a parking district or assessment district
- Bicycle parking substitution up to a capped percentage (`TBD — verify` `needs-verification`)
- Variance through the Board of Adjustment for parcel-specific hardship (see [[Mesa Variance]])
