# Mesa Landscape Standards

## Summary

Mesa's landscape standards govern the amount, placement, and type of landscaping required for new commercial, industrial, and multifamily development. In the Sonoran Desert climate, Mesa's landscape ordinance emphasizes water efficiency — requiring xeriscape-compatible plant palettes, drip or low-volume irrigation, and limiting high-water-use turf. Landscape standards address streetscape buffers along arterials and collectors, perimeter buffers between incompatible uses, parking lot interior landscaping, and foundation/entryway plantings. These standards are reviewed and enforced through the site plan review process.

Compliance with Mesa's landscape standards is evaluated at site plan review and inspected at certificate of occupancy. Projects that under-landscape or propose non-compliant plant species trigger plan review comments and resubmittal cycles.

## Applicability

Applies to all new commercial, industrial, and multifamily development, and to existing development when a threshold of expansion or redevelopment is exceeded. Single-family residential landscaping is subject to separate residential standards. Overlay districts (particularly Downtown and transit areas) may modify standard requirements. Variances from landscape standards may be sought through the Board of Adjustment (see [[Mesa Variance]]). Administrative adjustments for minor deviations may be available through Development Services.
