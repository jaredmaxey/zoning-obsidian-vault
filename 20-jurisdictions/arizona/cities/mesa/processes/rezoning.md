# Mesa Rezoning

## Summary

A rezoning (zone change) in Mesa changes the base zoning district classification on a specific parcel or group of parcels. Because zoning designations carry the force of law, a rezoning is a legislative action — the Planning and Zoning Board holds a public hearing and makes a recommendation, and the City Council takes final action. Most significant commercial and mixed-use development projects in Mesa that involve a change from one base zone to another require a rezoning. See [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] for cross-jurisdictional context.

If the proposed zone is inconsistent with the current General Plan land-use designation, a concurrent [[Mesa General Plan Amendment]] will also be required, adding process complexity and time. Applicants should confirm General Plan consistency at the pre-application stage to understand the full scope of entitlement needed.

## Triggering Conditions

A rezoning is required any time a property owner or developer seeks to change the zoning district designation on a parcel — for example, from a residential designation to a commercial or mixed-use designation, or from a less intensive commercial category to a higher-intensity commercial or industrial category. It is also required to implement a planned development or to establish a site-specific development agreement tied to a specific zone. Redevelopment projects that rely on uses not permitted in the existing zone must rezone before obtaining building permits.

## Decision-Maker

The **Planning and Zoning Board** holds the public hearing and makes a recommendation (approval, denial, or approval with conditions) to the **City Council**. The City Council takes final legislative action. Staff analysis and recommendation is provided in a staff report. ## Findings Required

Mesa's rezoning findings typically require a showing that the proposed zone change is consistent with the General Plan, compatible with surrounding land uses and the character of the area, serves the public interest, and does not adversely affect adjacent properties. ## Appeal Path

As a legislative action, a City Council rezoning decision is generally challenged in Maricopa County Superior Court (special action) rather than through an administrative appeal board. ## Common Pitfalls

- **General Plan inconsistency undetected:** Proceeding to design and application without confirming that the target zone is consistent with the General Plan can trigger a mandatory GPA and add months to the timeline.
- **Inadequate conceptual plan:** Boards and Council expect a credible development concept; a vague plan invites conditions or denial.
- **Neighbor opposition:** Mesa's Planning and Zoning Board is sensitive to neighborhood comments, particularly in transitional areas between residential and commercial. Early outreach reduces hearing-day surprises.
- **Traffic study delays:** Mesa requires traffic studies for many significant rezonings; consultant lead time should be built into the project schedule.
