# Mesa General Plan Amendment

## Summary

A General Plan Amendment (GPA) changes the land-use designation on Mesa's General Plan map or amends the text of an adopted General Plan policy. Because the General Plan is a legislative document, a GPA is a legislative action — it does not grant development rights by itself, but it realigns the policy foundation that guides subsequent rezoning and entitlement decisions. In practice, many significant rezonings in Mesa require a concurrent GPA when the proposed zone is inconsistent with the current General Plan designation. See also [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] for cross-jurisdictional context.

GPAs are typically batched into a docket cycle (the City processes amendments on a periodic schedule rather than on a rolling basis — `TBD — verify current docket schedule/frequency` `needs-verification`). Large or complex projects should confirm whether a GPA is necessary before investing in full entitlement studies, and should time their submittal to align with the docket cycle to avoid a full-cycle delay.

## Triggering Conditions

A GPA is required when a proposed project or rezoning would be inconsistent with the existing General Plan land-use designation for the subject property. Triggers include seeking a zone that permits uses or densities not contemplated by the current plan designation, or policy-level changes to circulation, open space, or other plan elements. Staff will evaluate consistency at pre-application; applicants should request a pre-app meeting to confirm whether a GPA is needed concurrently with a zone change.

## Decision-Maker

The **City Council** makes the final legislative decision on a GPA. The **Planning and Zoning Board** holds a public hearing and provides a recommendation to Council. ## Findings Required

For a GPA approval, Council must generally find that the amendment is consistent with the overall goals and policies of the General Plan, is compatible with surrounding land uses, and serves the public interest. ## Appeal Path

As a legislative act, a GPA decision by City Council is generally not subject to the standard quasi-judicial appeal process. Challenges are typically pursued in Maricopa County Superior Court by parties with standing. (`TBD — verify Mesa's specific GPA appeal procedures` `needs-verification`)

## Common Pitfalls

- **Missing the docket window:** GPAs processed on a cycle can add months if a submittal misses the intake deadline. Confirm the current docket schedule at pre-application.
- **Inconsistency not identified early:** Projects that proceed through design without confirming General Plan consistency may face a more complex and lengthy entitlement path.
- **Policy narrative insufficient:** The findings narrative must tie the proposed designation change to adopted General Plan goals; a generic narrative rarely succeeds.
- **Concurrent rezoning complexity:** A combined GPA + rezoning requires coordinating two separate approval actions, increasing the hearing schedule complexity and political exposure.
