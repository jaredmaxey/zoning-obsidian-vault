# Mesa Site Plan Review

## Summary

Site plan review in Mesa is an administrative review process through which Development Services evaluates a proposed development project's site layout, building placement, access, parking, landscaping, drainage, utilities, and other development standards before a building permit is issued. For many by-right commercial and multifamily projects, site plan review is entirely administrative — staff approves or conditionally approves without a public hearing. Certain project types or overlay district requirements may trigger design review or Planning and Zoning Board involvement. See [[Site Plan Review]] for cross-jurisdictional context.

Site plan review is the primary checkpoint where a project's compliance with all applicable development standards — parking, landscaping, lighting, signage, walls/fences, and setbacks — is confirmed. A complete, code-compliant submittal significantly reduces review cycles and speeds project delivery.

## Triggering Conditions

Site plan review is typically required for all new commercial, industrial, and multifamily development, as well as significant expansions of existing uses. Minor improvements, interior remodels, or tenant improvements below a threshold may follow a simpler building-permit-only path. ## Decision-Maker

For most projects, **Development Services staff** reviews and approves site plans administratively. Projects within certain overlay districts (e.g., Downtown), larger planned developments, or projects requiring a concurrent discretionary approval may involve the **Planning and Zoning Board** or a design review committee. ## Findings Required

Administrative site plan review is a standards-compliance determination rather than a discretionary findings exercise. Staff approves if the plan complies with all applicable zoning standards, development standards, and engineering requirements. ## Common Pitfalls

- **Incomplete first submittal:** Missing plan sheets (landscape, lighting, drainage) trigger automatic incompleteness and delay the review clock start.
- **Standards non-compliance in parking or landscaping:** Parking counts, aisle widths, and landscaping area calculations are the most common first-cycle comment items; resolve these with the code before submitting.
- **Overlay district requirements overlooked:** Projects in the Downtown or transit overlay areas must satisfy additional design standards; failure to address these at first submittal adds full review cycles.
- **Concurrent permit coordination:** Site plan approval and building permit submittal are separate tracks; staggering them appropriately avoids waiting on one for the other.
