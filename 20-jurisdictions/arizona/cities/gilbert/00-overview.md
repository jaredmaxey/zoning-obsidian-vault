# Gilbert, Arizona

## Overview

The Town of Gilbert is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in the United States, transforming over the past four decades from an agricultural community known as the "Hay Capital of the World" into a major East Valley suburb within the Phoenix metropolitan area. Despite its large population and economic activity, Gilbert retains its legal designation as a **Town** (not a City) under Arizona law — an important distinction for understanding its governing structure, though it functions in every practical respect as a full-service municipality.

Gilbert's development environment is characterized by a generally business-friendly posture, a strong emphasis on quality commercial and mixed-use development along major corridors, and growing investment in walkable, activated urban districts — particularly in and around Heritage District (downtown Gilbert). The town has a significant employment land base and has positioned itself as an innovation and technology destination in addition to its traditional residential growth role.

## Governing Body

Gilbert is governed under a **Council-Manager** form of government. The **Town Council** is the legislative body and ultimate land-use authority for major entitlement actions. The **Planning Commission** serves as the primary quasi-judicial and advisory body for rezonings, conditional use permits, variances, and similar discretionary actions — with its recommendations typically forwarded to Council for final action on legislative matters. The **Board of Adjustment** (or equivalent body — TBD, verify against current Gilbert Code) hears variance applications and certain appeals. Confirm current composition, quorum requirements, and specific jurisdictional boundaries of each body against the Gilbert Land Development Code (needs-verification).

## Planning Department

**Development Services Department**, Town of Gilbert. Administers zoning, entitlement review, building permits, code enforcement, and long-range planning. Primary public-facing portal: [https://www.gilbertaz.gov/government/departments/development-services](https://www.gilbertaz.gov/government/departments/development-services).

## General Plan / Comprehensive Plan

Gilbert operates under an adopted **General Plan** that serves as the town's long-range land-use and policy framework, as required under Arizona Revised Statutes Title 9. The plan establishes land-use designations, transportation corridors, and community character goals that guide both private development and public infrastructure investment. The current plan's title, adopted date, and horizon year should be confirmed against the Development Services website (needs-verification). Amendments to the General Plan require a supermajority vote of the Town Council and are subject to the GPA process described in [[Gilbert General Plan Amendment]].
