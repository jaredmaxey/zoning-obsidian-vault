# Gilbert Employment Corridor Overlay

## Summary

Gilbert has actively positioned itself as an employment and innovation destination in the Southeast Valley, attracting technology, healthcare, advanced manufacturing, and corporate office users. To protect and enhance the quality of development along its major employment corridors — including areas around Williams Gateway / Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport, major arterial corridors, and designated innovation zones — the town may maintain one or more overlay districts or specific-plan areas that impose elevated design standards, restrict incompatible uses, and promote high-quality built environments appropriate for attracting employers.

The exact name, boundaries, and provisions of any employment corridor overlay or its functional equivalent (which may instead be implemented through a Specific Plan, PUD standards, or base employment zone provisions rather than a separate overlay) should be confirmed against the current Gilbert LDC and General Plan (needs-verification). ## Geographic Extent

Likely applies to major employment and commercial corridors, potentially including: areas along the US 60 (Superstition Freeway) corridor; the Santan Village / SanTan Freeway (Loop 202) area; the Gateway Employment Area near Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport; and other designated employment nodes identified in the General Plan. ## Modifications to Base Zoning

Employment corridor overlay provisions, where they exist, commonly modify base zone standards to: raise minimum building design and landscaping quality; limit or regulate uses that are incompatible with employment character (certain retail, residential, or auto-oriented uses); establish build-to-line or setback standards that create a coherent streetscape; impose minimum building size or FAR requirements to prevent under-utilization of employment land; and require coordinated signage and lighting. Verify exact modifications in the LDC (needs-verification).

## Additional Requirements

May include: enhanced landscaping and tree coverage standards; coordinated monument signage criteria; minimum transparency requirements for office buildings; restrictions on outdoor storage; and loading/service area screening requirements. Verify complete requirements in the Gilbert LDC (needs-verification).

## Process Changes

Development within an employment corridor overlay may require additional design review steps or pre-application consultation with Development Services staff. Gateway parcels and major visible sites along freeway corridors may be subject to enhanced review. Confirm applicable process modifications in the LDC (needs-verification).
