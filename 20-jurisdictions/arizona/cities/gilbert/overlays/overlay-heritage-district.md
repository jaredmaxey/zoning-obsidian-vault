# Gilbert Heritage District Overlay

## Summary

The Heritage District is downtown Gilbert's historic core — a walkable, mixed-use neighborhood centered around the original town center that has emerged as one of the most economically vibrant and nationally recognized downtown districts of any municipality Gilbert's size. The Heritage District Overlay imposes additional design standards and review requirements on top of the underlying base zones to protect and enhance the district's distinctive character: human-scale building frontages, active ground-floor uses, pedestrian-oriented site design, and architecturally compatible materials and massing.

Development activity in the Heritage District is substantial and ongoing, with restaurants, retail, entertainment, residential, and office uses co-existing in a high-demand, low-vacancy environment. The overlay is the primary tool ensuring that new development reinforces rather than detracts from this character. Specific overlay provisions — including precise boundaries, permitted uses, design standards, sign criteria, and the review process — should be confirmed against the current Gilbert LDC and any adopted Heritage District Design Guidelines (needs-verification).

## Geographic Extent

The Heritage District Overlay covers the area in and around Gilbert's historic downtown core. Precise boundaries are mapped in the official Gilbert zoning map and LDC overlay chapter. ## Modifications to Base Zoning

The Heritage District Overlay typically modifies base zone standards in the following ways (verify each against current LDC — needs-verification):

- **Build-to lines / reduced setbacks:** buildings may be required or encouraged to be built close to the street to maintain a continuous, active street wall rather than set back behind parking.
- **Parking:** reduced minimum parking requirements or shared parking opportunities may be available within the overlay, recognizing the district's walkable character and availability of public/shared parking.
- **Active ground-floor uses:** retail, restaurant, and other pedestrian-generating uses may be required or preferred at ground level along primary streets.
- **Height:** the overlay may set specific height envelopes — potentially both minimums (to ensure urban-scale massing) and maximums (to protect the district's human-scale character) — verify against LDC (needs-verification).
- **Façade design:** building articulation, transparency (window-to-wall ratios), material standards, and architectural compatibility requirements typically apply and are more prescriptive than the base zone.

## Additional Requirements

The overlay likely imposes additional requirements for: signage (more architecturally integrated, smaller, less internally illuminated than standard commercial signs — see [[Gilbert Signage Standards]]); awnings and canopies; outdoor dining and activation; mechanical equipment screening; and public-realm improvements (sidewalk widths, street furniture, lighting fixtures). Verify complete list of additional requirements in the LDC and any adopted Heritage District Design Guidelines (needs-verification).

## Process Changes

Development within the Heritage District Overlay typically requires **design review** — either by Development Services staff using adopted design guidelines or by the Planning Commission — in addition to the standard site plan review. Pre-application meetings with Development Services are especially valuable in this overlay to understand current design priorities and avoid costly redesigns during review. Verify whether a separate Heritage District or design review board exists (needs-verification).
