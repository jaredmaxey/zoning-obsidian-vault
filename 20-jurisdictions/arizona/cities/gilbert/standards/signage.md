# Gilbert Signage Standards

## Summary

The Town of Gilbert's sign regulations govern the type, size, height, number, illumination, and design of commercial signage on private property. Like most municipalities, Gilbert distinguishes among major sign categories — monument signs (ground-mounted), wall signs (building-mounted), and directional/wayfinding signs — and sets maximums for each within specific zone categories. Sign permits are required for most permanent and temporary signs; sign criteria may be further refined in a Sign Criteria Plan submitted as part of a master development approval. See also [[Gilbert Heritage District Overlay]], which typically imposes more architecturally compatible sign design standards than the base zone sign code.

Sign regulations in Gilbert must comply with the U.S. Supreme Court's Reed v. Town of Gilbert (2015) decision — a landmark content-neutrality ruling that originated in this jurisdiction — meaning sign regulations cannot distinguish between sign types based on the message the sign conveys; regulations must be facially content-neutral (verify that current LDC reflects post-Reed compliance — needs-verification).

## Applicability

Sign standards apply to all new signs and changes to existing signs requiring a permit. Exempt signs (certain temporary signs, real estate signs, address numbers, etc.) are typically enumerated in the LDC. Electronic message centers, animated signs, and digital billboards are subject to additional standards — verify against current LDC (needs-verification).

## Exceptions

Master Sign Programs / Sign Criteria Plans may be approved as part of a PUD or development plan to allow coordinated signage that departs from standard maximums in a unified, aesthetically consistent manner. Variances from sign standards are available through the standard variance process (see [[Gilbert Variance]]). Heritage District design review may apply different approval criteria for signs within that overlay.

## Calculation Examples

*Illustrative only.* For a multi-tenant strip retail center, each tenant typically receives a wall sign allocation calculated as a percentage of the tenant's frontage or based on a per-linear-foot formula. A monument sign for the center would be allocated based on street frontage, with panel allocation divided among tenants. Verify specific formulas in the LDC (needs-verification).

## Related Zones

Sign standards apply across commercial, industrial, and mixed-use zones, with Heritage District overlay area having additional design review requirements. Related: [[Gilbert Heritage District Overlay]].
