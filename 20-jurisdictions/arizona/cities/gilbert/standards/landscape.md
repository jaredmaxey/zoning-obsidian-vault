# Gilbert Landscape Standards

## Summary

The Town of Gilbert's landscape standards govern the quantity, placement, and species of plant material required for commercial, industrial, and multi-family development. Given Gilbert's desert climate, the standards reflect a strong emphasis on **water-efficient, xeriscape-compatible plantings** — drought-tolerant and native or adaptive species are favored over high-water-use turf. Landscaping requirements apply to perimeter buffers (especially at residential/commercial interfaces), parking lot interiors, streetscapes along public rights-of-way, and project entries. Heritage District and other overlay areas may impose additional streetscape and tree standards.

Landscaping functions both aesthetically and functionally in Gilbert's code: it screens parking and service areas from street view, provides shade, manages stormwater, and creates visual separation between incompatible uses. Landscape plan approval is part of the site plan review process and a prerequisite for certificate of occupancy.

## Applicability

Landscape standards apply to new commercial, industrial, and multi-family development and to significant expansions or remodels that trigger compliance review. Specific thresholds for when landscape upgrades are required on existing sites should be confirmed in the Gilbert LDC (needs-verification). Single-family residential landscaping is addressed through separate residential standards, not this commercial standard.

## Exceptions

Administrative adjustments or variances to landscape requirements may be available for constrained sites where full compliance is physically impractical. Alternative landscape plans (using equivalent plant material in a different configuration) may be approved by Development Services. Heritage District and other overlays may have separate design review criteria that supersede base landscape minimums in their areas (needs-verification).
