# Gilbert Parking Standards

## Summary

The Town of Gilbert's off-street parking standards establish minimum vehicle parking requirements for all new construction and changes of use, as well as dimensional standards for parking stalls, drive aisles, and access. These standards are codified in the Gilbert Land Development Code and apply across the town, with modified requirements possible in specific overlay districts (particularly the Heritage District, where reduced or shared parking may be available). The standards are administered through the site plan review and building permit processes. See [[Parking Ratios by Use]] for cross-jurisdictional parking ratio comparisons.

Parking in Gilbert reflects the town's predominantly suburban character: most commercial zones require on-site surface parking, and minimum ratios by use type are the regulatory floor. Bicycle parking, ADA-accessible spaces, and EV charging provisions may also apply — verify current requirements (needs-verification).

## Applicability

Gilbert's parking standards apply to all new development, additions that increase gross floor area or intensity of use, and changes of use that result in a higher parking demand. Existing legal nonconforming parking situations may be grandfathered under specific conditions — verify in the Gilbert LDC (needs-verification). Overlay district provisions (e.g., Heritage District) may modify or relax minimum requirements within their boundaries.

## Exceptions

The Gilbert LDC may provide for: shared parking agreements between adjacent uses with offset peak-demand periods; parking reductions for mixed-use developments; administrative adjustments or variances for constrained sites; and reduced minimums within the Heritage District or other overlay areas. In-lieu fee programs (if any) should be verified against current LDC provisions (needs-verification).
