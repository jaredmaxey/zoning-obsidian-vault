# Gilbert SF-D

## Summary

The Single Family Detached (SF-D) district is a single-family residential zone for detached dwellings that is not identified by a specific minimum lot size number in the way that SF-6 through SF-43 are. SF-D is widely mapped across Gilbert and is very commonly paired with a Planned Area Development (PAD) overlay, which then specifies the actual lot size, setback, and coverage standards for each master-planned community. As a base zone, SF-D establishes the single-family detached character of development while deferring dimensional specifics to the PAD conditions. Verify exact base-zone standards applicable in the absence of a PAD against the LDC.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by Gilbert Land Development Code Chapter III (Subdivision Regulations). SF-D lots within PAD-governed communities are typically platted under the PAD preliminary development plan standards. Verify against [[Gilbert Subdivision Platting]].

## Overlay Interactions

PAD overlay is extremely common over SF-D parcels and will typically govern most development standards. The Santan Freeway Corridor Overlay and Vertical Development Overlay may also apply in specific locations.
