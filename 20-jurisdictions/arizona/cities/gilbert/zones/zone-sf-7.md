# Gilbert SF-7

## Summary

The Single Family-7 (SF-7) district is Gilbert's most widely-mapped single-family residential zone, requiring a minimum lot size of approximately 7,000 square feet. It dominates the town's established suburban neighborhoods and produces the characteristic detached single-family subdivision fabric associated with Gilbert's rapid 1990s–2010s growth. SF-7 is found in large, cohesive residential areas throughout the town's core, mid-sections, and newer subdivisions alike. Development consists exclusively of detached single-family homes with accessory uses and limited conditional approvals.

## Subdivision Standards

Governed by Gilbert Land Development Code Chapter III (Subdivision Regulations). SF-7 is the dominant subdivision lot standard across most of Gilbert's master-planned communities. Verify against [[Gilbert Subdivision Platting]].

## Overlay Interactions

PAD overlay is extremely common over SF-7 base zoning in Gilbert's master-planned communities. Verify PAD-specific modifications for any given project. Santan Freeway Corridor Overlay and Vertical Development Overlay may also apply.
