# Tempe Rezoning / Map Amendment

## Summary

A rezoning (map amendment) changes the official zoning classification of one or more parcels from one base zone district to another. In Tempe — a fully built-out city focused on infill and redevelopment — rezonings are among the most common and consequential entitlement actions because they unlock higher density, additional use types, or urban forms not permitted under legacy zoning. A rezoning is a legislative action decided by the Tempe City Council. Under Arizona law, the new zoning must be consistent with the adopted General Plan; if the proposal conflicts with the current General Plan land-use designation, a [[Tempe General Plan Amendment]] must be processed concurrently or first. See also: [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]].

## Triggering Conditions

A rezoning is required whenever a property owner proposes to develop or use a parcel in a manner not permitted under the current base zone. Common triggers include: redevelopment of a commercial corridor site to a mixed-use or higher-density residential zone; conversion of industrial land to office/R&D or residential; and infill development requiring a zone that permits greater FAR, height, or density. Tempe also uses planned area development (PAD) or equivalent planned district frameworks for larger assemblages — verify applicable zone designations in the current ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Decision-Maker

**Tempe City Council** makes the final legislative decision. A staff report and recommendation from the Community Development Department, plus a public hearing before the Development Review Commission (or equivalent advisory body), typically precede the Council hearing. ## Findings Required

As a legislative action the City Council has broad discretion. Typical considerations include: (1) consistency with the adopted General Plan land-use designation and policies; (2) compatibility with surrounding land uses and the character of the neighborhood; (3) whether the site is suitable for the proposed zoning given its size, shape, and infrastructure capacity; and (4) absence of a significant adverse impact on public health, safety, or welfare. ## Appeal Path

City Council rezoning decisions are legislative; challenges are made in the **Superior Court of Maricopa County**. Verify any internal reconsideration provision and applicable filing deadlines. (`needs-verification`)

## Common Pitfalls

- Misalignment between the proposed zone and the General Plan land-use designation — confirm General Plan consistency before application or plan a concurrent GPA.
- Inadequate community engagement; Tempe's infill/redevelopment projects often face neighbor scrutiny. Early and documented outreach reduces hearing-day surprises. See [[Neighborhood Outreach]] and [[Public Hearing Process]].
- Overlooking transit-overlay and TOD framework applicability, which may offer superior entitlements for sites near light-rail stations.
- For large or complex sites, underestimating the value of a pre-application conference with Community Development staff to align on submittal requirements.
