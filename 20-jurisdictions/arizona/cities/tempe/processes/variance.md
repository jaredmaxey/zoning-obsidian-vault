# Tempe Variance

## Summary

A Variance grants relief from the strict dimensional or development standards of the Zoning and Development Code when literal application of those standards would impose an unnecessary hardship arising from unique physical characteristics of the property — not merely because the standard is inconvenient or economically burdensome. In Tempe, variances are quasi-judicial decisions: the applicant bears the burden of demonstrating that specific findings can be made. The variance power is narrow by design; it does not allow a change in permitted use (that requires a Use Permit or Rezoning) and it does not run to self-created hardships. See also: [[Variance]].

## Triggering Conditions

A variance is appropriate when a parcel's unusual physical characteristics — such as irregular shape, exceptional narrowness or shallowness, topographic constraints, or an encumbrance not of the owner's making — make strict compliance with a dimensional standard (setback, height, lot coverage, parking, etc.) impractical or impossible. The hardship must be unique to the property, not general to the neighborhood. In Tempe's fully built-out, infill context, variance requests frequently arise from irregular lot shapes in older subdivisions, legally nonconforming structures, and redevelopment parcels with legacy encumbrances.

## Findings Required

Arizona variance law (ARS Title 9) and local ZDC provisions require the BOA to make affirmative findings (verify exact language in the ZDC — `needs-verification`) along lines such as: (1) there are special circumstances or conditions applicable to the property — not general to the neighborhood — arising from size, shape, topography, or location; (2) the variance is necessary for the preservation and enjoyment of substantial property rights; (3) granting the variance will not be materially detrimental to the public welfare or to adjacent properties; and (4) the hardship is not the result of actions by the applicant or prior owners. Failure to satisfy all required findings is grounds for denial.

## Appeal Path

BOA decisions on variances are typically challenged by **special action in the Superior Court of Maricopa County**; administrative appeal within City government is generally not available for BOA decisions. Verify filing deadline. (`needs-verification`) See also: [[Appeals Process]].

## Common Pitfalls

- Confusing variance with use permit or rezoning — variances are strictly for dimensional/development standards relief, not use changes.
- Self-created hardship defeats a variance claim; any constraint resulting from the applicant's own subdivision, development, or design decisions will not support approval.
- Weak findings narrative: the legal standard is fact-specific — generic statements about inconvenience or cost are insufficient.
- Failing to document the physical site condition (survey, photos) that creates the hardship; the record must support the findings.
