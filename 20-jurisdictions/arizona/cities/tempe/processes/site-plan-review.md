# Tempe Development Plan Review

## Summary

Tempe's Development Plan Review (the local term for what is broadly called Site Plan Review) is the process by which the city evaluates a proposed project's site layout, architecture, landscaping, parking and circulation, lighting, and compatibility with adjacent uses and the surrounding neighborhood before issuing a building permit. Unlike a rezoning (which is legislative), a Development Plan Review is an administrative or quasi-judicial review applying objective or discretionary design and development standards. For most commercial, multi-family, and mixed-use development in Tempe, some form of development plan review is required as a condition of building permit approval. See also: [[Site Plan Review]].

## Triggering Conditions

Development plan review is typically required for: new construction of commercial, office, industrial, multi-family residential, or mixed-use buildings; substantial additions or expansions of non-single-family structures; changes of use that trigger additional development standards; and any project subject to a Use Permit or rezoning condition requiring plan approval. Thresholds distinguishing administrative (staff-level) review from full DRC public hearing review should be verified against the current ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Decision-Maker

Projects above the administrative threshold are reviewed by the **Development Review Commission (DRC)** at a public hearing. Smaller or less complex projects may be approved administratively by Community Development staff. Verify current thresholds and any delegation of authority in the ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Findings Required

For DRC-level reviews, the commission typically evaluates: (1) compliance with applicable development standards (setbacks, height, parking, landscaping, lighting); (2) architectural compatibility with the surrounding context; (3) adequacy of internal circulation and access; (4) mitigation of impacts on adjacent properties; and (5) consistency with the General Plan and any applicable overlay district standards. ## Appeal Path

DRC development plan decisions may be appealed to the **City Council** or **Board of Adjustment** depending on the nature of the decision — verify the current appeal path and deadline in the ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Common Pitfalls

- Underestimating the design-review rigor in Tempe — particularly in the Downtown/Mill Avenue corridor and near ASU, the DRC evaluates architecture and urban design substantively, not just dimensional compliance.
- Submitting incomplete or uncoordinated plan sets (site plan inconsistent with landscape plan or elevations); completeness check rejections delay the clock.
- Not addressing lighting and photometric compliance upfront, particularly for projects near residential uses.
- Failure to engage adjacent property owners and neighbors before the DRC hearing in infill contexts where compatibility questions are likely.
