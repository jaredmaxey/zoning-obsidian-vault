# Tempe, Arizona

## Overview

Tempe is a fully built-out, landlocked city of approximately 40 square miles in the east-central core of the Phoenix metropolitan area (Maricopa County). Because there is no vacant peripheral land to absorb growth, Tempe's development market is defined almost entirely by infill redevelopment, adaptive reuse, and densification of aging commercial corridors and surface parking lots. The city's proximity to Arizona State University (ASU) — one of the largest public universities in the United States, with its main campus spanning central Tempe — shapes land use demand toward higher-density residential, student housing, hospitality, and innovation-oriented office and R&D uses.

Tempe is also a primary node on the Valley Metro Light Rail system and the associated Tempe Streetcar, making it one of the most transit-accessible jurisdictions in the metropolitan area. This transit infrastructure underpins the city's transit-oriented development (TOD) policies and has driven a wave of high-density mixed-use projects along the light-rail corridor, particularly around Tempe Town Lake, the Rio Salado waterfront, and the Downtown / Mill Avenue submarket. The city's zoning approach reflects this context: reduced parking requirements near transit, incentives for walkable urban form, and a General Plan that prioritizes intensification over sprawl.
