# Tempe Parking Standards

## Summary

Tempe's off-street parking standards establish minimum (and in some areas, maximum) parking supply requirements by use type and zone. Tempe occupies a distinctive position in the Phoenix metro: as a transit-rich, fully built-out city served by the Valley Metro Light Rail and Tempe Streetcar, the city has reduced or eliminated parking minimums in transit-proximate areas to encourage TOD, activate the pedestrian realm, and make infill redevelopment economically viable. This makes Tempe's parking regime materially different from suburban Arizona jurisdictions. The specific parking table, including any TOD or downtown reductions, is codified in the Tempe ZDC — exact ratios require verification against current code. See also: [[Parking Ratios by Use]].

## Applicability

Parking standards apply to all new construction, changes of use, and additions in Tempe that trigger parking review under the ZDC. In transit-oriented overlay areas (near light-rail stations) and the Downtown/Mill Avenue overlay, reduced minimums or parking maximum caps may apply. Bicycle parking requirements typically accompany vehicle parking standards. Verify zone-by-zone and overlay-specific applicability in the ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Exceptions

Tempe's ZDC may permit: parking reductions for shared parking arrangements; reductions for proximity to transit; reductions via parking study (if actual demand is demonstrably lower); in-lieu fee payments for parking in certain districts; and administrative adjustments.
