# Tempe Landscape Standards

## Summary

Tempe's landscape standards govern the amount, placement, species selection, and irrigation requirements for vegetation on developed sites. In Tempe's desert climate, these standards serve dual purposes: aesthetic quality and urban heat island mitigation. The city's landscape requirements typically address perimeter buffers along streets and property lines, parking lot shading (trees required within and around surface parking areas), water efficiency (xeric or low-water-use plant palettes preferred or required), and maintenance standards. Exact ratios, species lists, and coverage percentages are codified in the ZDC and must be verified against current code. (`needs-verification`)

## Applicability

Landscape standards typically apply to all new non-single-family development and major additions in Tempe. Single-family residential landscape requirements may differ or be separately governed. Verify zone-by-zone applicability, any exemptions for small projects, and how the standards interact with overlay districts (particularly Downtown/Mill Avenue, where urban hardscape may substitute for traditional landscaping).
