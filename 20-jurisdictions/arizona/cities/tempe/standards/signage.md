# Tempe Signage Standards

## Summary

Tempe's sign code (typically a chapter or article of the ZDC or a stand-alone sign ordinance) regulates the size, height, placement, number, and illumination of signs on commercial, multi-family, and mixed-use properties. The standards balance wayfinding and business identification needs against aesthetic quality and visual clutter concerns — particularly important in Tempe's active retail corridors, the Downtown/Mill Avenue entertainment district, and ASU-adjacent areas. Electronic message center (EMC) and digital sign regulations, which have been actively litigated and updated across Arizona municipalities, require special attention and current-code verification. Exact measurements and counts are TBD and must be confirmed against the current ZDC. (`needs-verification`)

## Applicability

Sign standards apply to all permanent and temporary signage on commercial, mixed-use, multi-family, and non-residential properties. Single-family residential sign requirements are typically more limited. Verify whether the Downtown/Mill Avenue overlay or other character-area overlays impose separate or modified sign standards.
