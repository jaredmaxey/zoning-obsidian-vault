# Tempe Lighting Standards

## Summary

Tempe's outdoor lighting standards regulate site illumination levels, light trespass onto adjacent properties, fixture type and mounting height, and compliance with Arizona's Outdoor Lighting Act (dark-sky requirements). Arizona is one of the few states with a statewide outdoor lighting statute (ARS Title 49, Article 15 — verify citation) designed to protect observatory conditions; Tempe's local standards must comply with or exceed this state framework. In practice, lighting design for commercial, multi-family, and mixed-use projects in Tempe requires a photometric plan demonstrating compliance with maximum footcandle levels, uniformity ratios, and light-trespass limits at property lines. Specific numeric thresholds are TBD and must be confirmed against the current ZDC and state statute. (`needs-verification`)

## Applicability

Lighting standards apply to all exterior site lighting for new commercial, industrial, multi-family, and mixed-use development, and to significant modifications of existing lighting systems. Residential single-family may have reduced requirements. Verify the specific trigger thresholds and any overlay-area modifications. (`needs-verification`)
