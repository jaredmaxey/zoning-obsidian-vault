# Tempe Overlay Districts Index

## Summary

Tempe maintains a set of overlay districts that modify or supplement the standards of underlying base zones in specific geographic areas or along specific corridors. Overlays in Tempe generally serve one of three purposes: (1) enabling transit-oriented or high-density development near light-rail stations and the Tempe Streetcar corridor; (2) enforcing enhanced design and use standards in the Downtown/Mill Avenue entertainment and urban core; and (3) protecting character areas or historic resources. The full overlay roster requires verification against the current Tempe Zoning and Development Code. (`needs-verification`)

## Modifications to Base Zoning

Each overlay modifies the base zone in distinct ways — see individual overlay notes for detail. In general, Tempe's transit and downtown overlays tend to expand permitted uses, reduce parking requirements, increase height allowances, and impose enhanced design standards relative to the underlying base zone.

## Additional Requirements

Overlays typically impose design review and compatibility requirements beyond those of the base zone. The Downtown/Mill Avenue overlay likely includes pedestrian-frontage standards, active ground-floor use requirements, and architectural compatibility review. The transportation/transit overlay likely includes TOD design principles such as building-to-sidewalk placement and bicycle infrastructure. Specifics require verification. (`needs-verification`)

## Process Changes

Projects within overlay areas typically require compliance with overlay standards in addition to base-zone standards, and may require DRC review of overlay compliance as part of the development plan review process. Historic overlay projects may require an additional historic preservation board or staff review. Verify per overlay. (`needs-verification`)
