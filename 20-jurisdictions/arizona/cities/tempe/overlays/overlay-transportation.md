# Tempe Transportation Overlay District

## Summary

The Tempe Transportation Overlay District (exact name TBD — verify against current ZDC; `needs-verification`) is an overlay framework applied to areas proximate to Valley Metro Light Rail stations and the Tempe Streetcar corridor. Its purpose is to enable and encourage transit-oriented development (TOD): higher-density, mixed-use, pedestrian-oriented projects that capitalize on transit access and reduce automobile dependence. Key modifications typically include reduced or eliminated parking minimums, increased height and density allowances above base-zone limits, and design standards that reinforce walkability (building placement at the sidewalk, active ground-floor uses, bicycle parking and infrastructure). This overlay is a primary mechanism through which Tempe has repositioned itself as one of the most TOD-friendly jurisdictions in the Phoenix metro.

## Geographic Extent

The overlay applies to parcels within a defined distance of light-rail station platforms along the Metro Light Rail alignment through central Tempe (generally following Apache Boulevard / University Drive and connecting to the Rio Salado / Tempe Town Lake area), and may extend along the Tempe Streetcar route. Exact station-area boundaries and walking-distance radii are defined in the ZDC and on the official zoning map — verify. (`needs-verification`)
