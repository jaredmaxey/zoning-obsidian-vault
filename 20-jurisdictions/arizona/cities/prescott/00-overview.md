# Prescott, Arizona

## Overview

Prescott is the county seat of Yavapai County and one of Arizona's most historically significant cities, situated at approximately 5,300 feet elevation in the central highlands about 100 miles north of Phoenix. The city is known for its Victorian-era architecture, Whiskey Row, Courthouse Plaza, the Sharlot Hall Museum, and a robust arts and cultural scene that anchors year-round tourism.

The city's economic base rests on tourism (heritage and outdoor recreation), healthcare (Yavapai Regional Medical Center and associated providers), higher education (Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, Prescott College, Yavapai College), and retiree in-migration from across the country attracted by the mild climate and small-city character. Population growth has been steady, driven largely by retirement and second-home demand.

For CRE, Prescott is a mature, constrained market rather than a high-growth frontier. The historic downtown and topographically varied terrain limit large-format retail, industrial, and high-density residential. Healthcare, senior housing, boutique retail, and hospitality are the most active CRE categories. ## Governing Body

Prescott operates under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings, general plan amendments, and conditional use permits. Prescott has an active historic preservation program that intersects with zoning decisions in the downtown core.
