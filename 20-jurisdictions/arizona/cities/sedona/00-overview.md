# Sedona, Arizona

## Overview

Sedona is one of Arizona's most recognized cities internationally, famous for its dramatic red-rock buttes, vortex tourism, and a thriving arts community. The city straddles two counties — the majority of Sedona lies in **Yavapai County**, with the northern portion (including the Village of Oak Creek area) in **Coconino County**. This dual-county jurisdiction is an important administrative fact: the city's boundaries cross county lines, though the City of Sedona itself administers a unified municipal code regardless of the underlying county.

Tourism and hospitality are the dominant economic drivers, with millions of visitors annually supporting a high concentration of resorts, boutique hotels, restaurants, retail galleries, and wellness/spa facilities. The residential market skews toward second homes, retirees, and high-income households.

Sedona's CRE market is extremely supply-constrained. The surrounding National Forest and BLM lands create a hard land boundary; the city is essentially built out with almost no greenfield opportunity. Entitlement activity is infill and redevelopment-focused, with high design standards, view-corridor protections, and community character requirements that reflect the city's world-class destination identity. Any [[Highest and Best Use Analysis]] must account for these constraints alongside premium hospitality and retail values. The [[Entitlement Play]] here is primarily adaptive reuse or land-use intensification, not greenfield development.

## Governing Body

Sedona operates under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings and general plan amendments. Sedona is known for a deliberate and community-engaged entitlement process reflecting the high value residents place on visual character.
