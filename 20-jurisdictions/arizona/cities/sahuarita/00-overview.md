# Sahuarita, Arizona

## Overview

Sahuarita is a Pima County town located south of Tucson in the Santa Cruz Valley, between Tucson proper and the border community of Nogales. The town is dominated by the large Rancho Sahuarita master-planned community, which has been the primary engine of the town's residential growth and provides much of its commercial activity. Freeport-McMoRan's open-pit copper mine (Sierrita mine) is a significant industrial and employment presence in the western portion of the Sahuarita area.

The town's CRE market is modest in scale relative to Tucson and the Phoenix metro. The Rancho Sahuarita development includes planned commercial nodes that support retail, restaurant, and service-oriented uses serving the residential community. Growth is steady but not at the pace of Phoenix West Valley peers. The town's location on the I-19 corridor (connecting Tucson to the US-Mexico border) adds a transportation and logistics dimension.

For CRE practitioners, Sahuarita is primarily a neighborhood/community retail and residential-serving commercial market, with limited industrial or office demand outside of mine-support uses. Entitlement activity is largely tied to the continuing build-out of Rancho Sahuarita and adjacent residential areas.
