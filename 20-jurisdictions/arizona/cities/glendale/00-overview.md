# Glendale, Arizona

## Overview

Glendale is a major West Valley city in Maricopa County, located immediately northwest of Phoenix. It is best known regionally for its sports and entertainment corridor anchored by State Farm Stadium (home of the NFL Arizona Cardinals and frequent Super Bowl host) and the adjacent Westgate Entertainment District, which draw millions of visitors annually. This hospitality, retail, and event-venue demand makes Glendale a prime market for hotel, restaurant, and entertainment-related CRE investment.

Beyond its sports identity, Glendale has a mature urban core with historic downtown shopping districts and a diversified economic base that includes healthcare, education, and light industrial uses. The city is largely built out in its eastern portions and pursues infill and redevelopment strategies in established areas, while some western annexation areas still offer greenfield opportunity.

For CRE purposes, the Westgate/Cardinals corridor commands premium attention for hospitality and retail plays, while infill residential and mixed-use near transit corridors are active entitlement themes. The city's general plan guides future land use and is the reference point for any [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] or [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]].

## Governing Body

The City of Glendale is governed by a Mayor and City Council. Land-use decisions flow through the Planning Commission, which makes recommendations, with the City Council serving as the final decision-making authority for rezonings, general plan amendments, and major entitlements. A Zoning Administrator handles administrative and minor determinations.
