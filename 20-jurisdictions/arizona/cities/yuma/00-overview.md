# Yuma, Arizona

## Overview

Yuma is the county seat of Yuma County in the extreme southwestern corner of Arizona, at the confluence of the Colorado and Gila Rivers near the borders of California and Mexico. The city's economy is anchored by three major pillars: agriculture (Yuma is the "Lettuce Capital of the World" and one of the nation's most productive winter vegetable growing regions), the military (Marine Corps Air Station Yuma, or MCAS Yuma, is one of the largest Marine Corps air stations in the world and a major economic force), and international trade through the Yuma port of entry.

The CRE market reflects these drivers. Agricultural processing, cold storage, and logistics facilities serving the farming sector are significant industrial uses. Military-adjacent retail, healthcare, and housing serve a large MCAS Yuma workforce. The international border connection supports retail demand from Mexican consumers and cross-border trade logistics.

Yuma is a steady, regionally important market rather than a high-growth frontier. CRE plays tend to be driven by agricultural supply-chain logistics, military-serving retail and residential, healthcare, and border-trade commercial. [[Land]] with agricultural-to-industrial or commercial conversion potential near logistics routes can represent a [[Highest and Best Use Analysis]] opportunity as the agricultural sector modernizes.

## Governing Body

Yuma operates under a Mayor-Council government with a professional City Manager. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings and general plan amendments.
