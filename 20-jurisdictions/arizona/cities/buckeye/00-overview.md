# Buckeye, Arizona

## Overview

Buckeye is located in the far West Valley of Maricopa County and is one of the largest cities by land area in the United States, encompassing a vast swath of desert terrain stretching well beyond its current urbanized footprint. It has ranked among the fastest-growing cities in the nation in recent census periods, driven by a surge of master-planned residential development and the westward expansion of the Phoenix metropolitan area.

The city's CRE story is fundamentally a long-runway greenfield narrative: enormous tracts of undeveloped land, expanding freeway access (I-10 corridor), aggressive annexation, and a population base that has created strong demand for retail, industrial, and mixed-use uses that have historically lagged rooftop growth. Industrial and logistics developers have targeted Buckeye for large-format facilities, following the I-10 industrial corridor established in Goodyear further east.

For CRE practitioners, Buckeye represents a high-upside, longer-timeline market where [[Land]] assembly, master-plan entitlement, and phased [[Subdivision Approval]] are the primary plays. Infrastructure availability — water, sewer, power — is a material underwriting variable given the scale of land involved. A [[Highest and Best Use Analysis]] must account for phasing risk and absorption timelines.

## Governing Body

Buckeye operates under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews land-use applications and makes recommendations; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings, general plan amendments, and major entitlements. Large annexed areas may require pre-zoning actions as part of the annexation process.
