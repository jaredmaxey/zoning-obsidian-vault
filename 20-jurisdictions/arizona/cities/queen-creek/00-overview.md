# Queen Creek, Arizona

## Overview

Queen Creek is a fast-growing town in the Southeast Valley of Maricopa County, situated at the far southeastern edge of the greater Phoenix metro. Incorporated as a town (not a city), it has transformed over the past two decades from an agricultural and equestrian community into a major destination for master-planned residential development, attracting families priced out of closer-in suburbs.

The town retains a distinct character — its equestrian lifestyle heritage, agritourism attractions (Schnepf Farms, Queen Creek Olive Mill), and spacious lot norms distinguish it from denser Valley submarkets. This posture translates to zoning that historically has been oriented toward large-lot residential and rural commercial, though the town has expanded its commercial and mixed-use designations as the population has grown.

For CRE, Queen Creek is a primarily residential-driven market with growing retail demand trailing rooftop growth. [[Land]] assembly for master-planned community projects and retail pad development are the primary commercial plays. [[Subdivision Approval]] activity is high. The town's planning is closely watched for infrastructure capacity and utility extension timelines.
