# Chandler, Arizona

## Overview

Chandler is a major employment and residential city in the southeast Valley of Maricopa County, Arizona. It is part of the Phoenix–Mesa–Chandler MSA and has experienced sustained high-growth development over several decades, transitioning from an agricultural economy to one anchored in technology, semiconductor manufacturing, and corporate headquarters. Intel operates major semiconductor fabrication facilities within Chandler's boundaries, and the Price Road Corridor has emerged as one of the most significant technology employment corridors in the American Southwest, attracting corporate campuses, R&D uses, and supporting mixed-use and commercial development.

Chandler's zoning and development-standards framework is governed by the Chandler Unified Development Manual (UDM) — a consolidated code integrating zoning, subdivision, and development standards into a single document. The city's General Plan guides long-range land use policy, with designations that map to UDM zoning districts. Chandler practices active planning through its Planning Division, and the city has invested in infill, redevelopment, and urban-form enhancement particularly in its historic downtown core and along major employment corridors.

## Governing Body

The **Chandler City Council** is the legislative authority for zoning matters, including General Plan amendments and rezonings. The **Planning & Zoning Commission** (P&Z Commission) serves as the primary quasi-judicial and advisory body, hearing applications for rezonings, conditional use permits, and subdivision plats before forwarding recommendations to Council. The **Board of Adjustment** hears variance requests and appeals from administrative decisions. ## Planning Department

The **City of Chandler Planning Division** administers land use entitlements, zoning inquiries, and development review. It operates under the broader Planning and Development Department. The Planning Division manages pre-application conferences, application processing, staff reports, and coordination with the Engineering, Fire, and other review departments. Primary web portal: https://www.chandleraz.gov/government/departments/planning-and-development/planning

## General Plan / Comprehensive Plan

Chandler has an adopted **General Plan** (most recently updated; exact horizon year TBD — verify against current General Plan adoption resolution). `needs-verification` The General Plan establishes future land use designations, transportation, infrastructure, and community character goals. In Arizona, a General Plan amendment (GPA) is required before a rezoning can be approved if the proposed zoning is inconsistent with the current General Plan designation — making GPAs the upstream policy lever for large-scale land use change. Chandler's General Plan reflects the city's mature build-out status in many areas while identifying redevelopment opportunity sites, mixed-use nodes, and employment growth areas along the Price Corridor and in downtown.
