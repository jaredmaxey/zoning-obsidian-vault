# Chandler AP-1

## Summary

The AP-1 Airport District is a specialized base zone governing the Chandler Municipal Airport (CHD) and Stellar Airpark, establishing land use permissions and development standards specific to aviation operations and airport-compatible development. AP-1 accommodates aviation-related uses — runways, taxiways, hangars, fixed base operators (FBOs), aircraft maintenance, fuel storage, and airport terminal facilities — while restricting non-aviation uses incompatible with airfield safety requirements.

Chandler Municipal Airport is a general aviation (GA) reliever airport within the Phoenix metro. Stellar Airpark is a private residential/industrial airpark. Both generate the Airport Impact Overlay (A.I.O.) district that extends beyond AP-1 into surrounding base zones. Airport-adjacent industrial and commercial development in AP-1 and the surrounding I-1/I-2 zones is a specialized real estate category requiring careful review of FAA Part 77 airspace regulations, Chandler Airport Land Use Compatibility Plan, and the A.I.O. overlay restrictions.

## Subdivision Standards

Airport land use is subject to FAA grant assurance requirements and the Airport Layout Plan (ALP). Subdivision within AP-1 must comply with FAA compatibility standards and Chandler Airport Land Use Compatibility Plan.

## Overlay Interactions

The Airport Impact Overlay (A.I.O.) extends from AP-1 outward into surrounding base zones, establishing four sub-areas (ANO-1, ANO-2, ANO-3, CZO) based on noise contours and clear zones. Development within AP-1 is also subject to FAA Part 77 airspace obstruction standards.
