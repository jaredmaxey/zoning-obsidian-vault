# Chandler I-1

## Summary

The I-1 Planned Industrial District is Chandler's planned (lighter) industrial zone, designed to accommodate manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, research and development, and office-industrial uses in a planned, design-conscious environment. The "planned" designation implies that I-1 development must adhere to coordinated site design standards — enhanced landscaping, architectural quality, and buffering requirements — distinguishing it from the more permissive I-2 General Industrial District.

I-1 is well-suited for flex/industrial, business park, and tech-manufacturing uses. Given Chandler's role as a center of semiconductor fabrication and advanced manufacturing (Intel FABs, Microchip Technology), I-1 and P.C.O. zones along the Price Corridor and near the Loop 101 are among the highest-value industrial land in the Phoenix MSA. Semiconductor fabrication facilities (fabs) typically require I-1 or PAD zoning with significant infrastructure capacity — water, power, and hazmat management provisions must be verified.

## Subdivision Standards

Industrial subdivision governed by Chandler UDM. Multi-tenant industrial parks and campus subdivisions require coordinated plat review with shared access and utility easements.

## Overlay Interactions

Airport Impact Overlay (A.I.O.) may affect I-1 parcels near Chandler Municipal Airport or Stellar Airpark, primarily influencing height, vertical structures, and certain occupancy types. Industrial uses are generally more compatible with airport proximity than residential.
