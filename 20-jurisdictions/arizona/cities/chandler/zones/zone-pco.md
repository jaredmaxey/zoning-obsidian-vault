# Chandler P.C.O.

## Summary

The P.C.O. Planned Commercial Office District is a specialized planned zone in Chandler that accommodates professional office, medical office, research and development (R&D), and compatible light commercial uses in a campus or corridor setting. Unlike the general commercial C-series districts, the P.C.O. designation implies a planned, design-conscious approach to office and commercial development with coordinated site planning, enhanced landscaping, and architectural coherence requirements.

The P.C.O. district is closely associated with Chandler's Price Road Corridor — one of the most significant technology employment corridors in the Southwest — where campus-style office and R&D facilities serve major employers including Intel, Microchip Technology, PayPal, and other technology companies. P.C.O. zoned properties are high-value employment land uses, and the district standards typically reflect a corporate campus aesthetic with generous setbacks, quality landscaping, and enhanced site improvements. Careful attention to parking ratios and FAR is essential for underwriting these properties.

## Subdivision Standards

Campus-style office subdivision governed by Chandler UDM. Multi-parcel campus environments may require reciprocal access, shared parking agreements, and cross-easement documentation.

## Overlay Interactions

Airport Impact Overlay (A.I.O.) may affect P.C.O. parcels near Chandler Municipal Airport. Given the P.C.O.'s association with office and R&D uses, noise and clear-zone restrictions would primarily affect ancillary residential or assembly uses.
