# Chandler PAD

## Summary

The Planned Area Development (PAD) is Chandler's master planned development designation, established in Chapter 35, Article XVII of the Chandler Municipal Code. Unlike standard base zones with fixed dimensional standards, PAD designation allows applicants to propose custom standards — uses, densities, heights, setbacks, parking, and design requirements — through a comprehensive master plan approved by the Planning & Zoning Commission and City Council. The PAD process enables flexibility for large, complex, or multi-phase projects that do not fit neatly into a standard base zone.

PAD zoning is the preferred mechanism for master-planned communities, corporate campuses, mixed-use developments, large-scale industrial parks, transit-oriented developments, and other projects requiring site-specific tailoring of standards. The PAD plan becomes the binding regulatory document for the designated area — it supersedes the underlying base zone standards and establishes the specific development envelope, permitted uses, and design guidelines. Any deviation from the approved PAD plan requires an amendment through the same approval process.

Chandler's tech corridor growth (Intel campus expansions, Microchip Technology campus) and mixed-use redevelopment projects have made PAD one of the most frequently used tools in the city's entitlement toolkit for large projects.

## Permitted Uses

PAD uses are established by the specific approved PAD master plan and are not fixed by the base code. Typical PAD applications cover:

| Use Category | Typical PAD Application | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mixed-use (residential + commercial) | Transit-oriented, downtown-adjacent | Uses and densities set by plan |
| Master-planned residential community | Large suburban subdivisions | Custom lot standards |
| Corporate / technology campus | Price Corridor, Intel environs | Custom FAR, height, parking |
| Mixed-use industrial / R&D park | Technology-industrial campus | Custom standards |
| Regional commercial center | Large retail / lifestyle center | Custom site plan |
| Senior living / specialty housing | Age-restricted community | Custom density |

## Subdivision Standards

PAD master plans typically include a phasing schedule and subdivision framework. Individual phase plats are processed through standard subdivision procedures — verify PAD amendment requirements for any changes to the master plan.

## Overlay Interactions

Overlays (A.I.O., A.R.O.D., MHOD) may still apply to PAD areas depending on geographic location. The PAD plan must address overlay requirements. Verify with the Planning Division.
