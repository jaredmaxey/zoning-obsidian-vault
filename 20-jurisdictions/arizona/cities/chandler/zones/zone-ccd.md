# Chandler CCD

## Summary

The City Center District (CCD) is Chandler's historic downtown zone, established to promote and protect the unique character, visual identity, and traditional commercial vitality of the downtown core surrounding A.J. Chandler Park. The CCD is a form-based and use-specific district that permits ground-floor specialty retail, restaurants, offices, personal services, and financial institutions, with upper-floor residential uses requiring a conditional use permit.

Based on fetched code content, CCD development standards include a zero-setback allowance from property lines (promoting a traditional street wall), a maximum building height of 30 feet (45 feet with a 45-degree angle slope), and up to 100% lot coverage — creating an urban, compact downtown block pattern. The district mandates Early Twentieth Century Commercial architectural style and requires Historic Preservation Commission design review. The CCD is geographically bounded to the properties fronting San Marcos Place, Arizona Place, Buffalo Street, Boston Street, and Arizona Avenue (between Chandler Boulevard and Buffalo Street) per the Official Zoning Map.

## Subdivision Standards

## Overlay Interactions

Historic Preservation Commission (HPC) design review is required for all CCD development per fetched code content. The Adaptive Reuse Overlay District (A.R.O.D.) may also apply to certain CCD properties eligible for historic building conversions.
