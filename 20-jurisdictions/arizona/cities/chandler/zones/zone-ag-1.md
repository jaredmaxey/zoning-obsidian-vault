# Chandler AG-1

## Summary

The AG-1 Agricultural District is Chandler's lowest-intensity base zone, established to preserve and accommodate farming, ranching, and agricultural uses on large tracts of land. It is applied to areas where agricultural activities are ongoing or where the city's General Plan anticipates long-term holding of rural character pending future urbanization. Given Chandler's mature build-out status, AG-1 zoning is increasingly rare within city limits and is most likely found at the city's periphery or in areas recently annexed from unincorporated Maricopa County.

AG-1 properties typically feature large minimum lot sizes, generous setbacks, and limited infrastructure requirements. The district accommodates customary agricultural operations — crop farming, animal husbandry, and associated structures — alongside low-density single-family residential uses. Future development often requires rezoning to a residential or commercial district consistent with the applicable General Plan designation.
