# Chandler Airport Environs Overlay

## Summary

Chandler Municipal Airport (CHD) is a general aviation reliever airport located within Chandler's boundaries. Arizona law (ARS Title 28 aviation statutes) and FAA regulations require municipalities to protect airport airspace from incompatible development, and Maricopa County maintains an Airport Land Use Compatibility Plan (ALUCP) that governs land use planning in the vicinity of airports countywide. Chandler's zoning code incorporates an airport environs overlay (exact title, UDM section, and boundary TBD — verify against current UDM and the Maricopa County ALUCP). `needs-verification` The overlay restricts building heights that could create FAA-reportable obstructions, limits noise-sensitive residential uses within high-noise contours, and may require avigation easements for new development. The exact name and UDM structure of this overlay — it may be called an "Airport Hazard Overlay," "Airport Influence Area," or similar — must be confirmed against current Chandler code. `needs-verification`

## Geographic Extent

The airport environs overlay boundary is defined by the airspace surfaces (approach, departure, transitional, horizontal, and conical surfaces) established in the FAA-approved Airport Layout Plan for Chandler Municipal Airport, plus any noise contour zones established in the ALUCP. - **Use restrictions:** Prohibitions or conditional limitations on noise-sensitive uses (schools, hospitals, high-density residential) within specified noise contour zones (typically 65+ dB CNEL/DNL).

- **Disclosure requirements:** Real estate transactions within the overlay area may require airport proximity disclosure.
