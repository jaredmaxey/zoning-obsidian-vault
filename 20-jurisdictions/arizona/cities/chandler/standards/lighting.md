# Chandler Lighting Standards

## Summary

Chandler's outdoor lighting standards within the UDM govern exterior illumination for commercial, industrial, and multifamily development with dual goals: ensuring adequate light levels for safety and security while limiting light trespass onto adjacent properties and light pollution into the night sky. Arizona, as a state with extensive observatory activity (particularly near Flagstaff), has a strong statewide culture of lighting standards aligned with dark-sky principles, and many municipalities including Chandler require full-cutoff or downward-directing fixtures that minimize upward light scatter. Photometric plans are required as part of the development plan review process.

## Applicability

Outdoor lighting standards apply to all new commercial, industrial, multifamily, and mixed-use development and to significant site modifications. Parking lot lighting, building-mounted security lighting, canopy lighting (fuel stations, drive-throughs), and pedestrian-area lighting are all regulated. Residential single-family lighting may follow separate or exempt standards (TBD — verify). `needs-verification`

## Exceptions

Light level reductions or waivers may be available for certain low-impact uses, infill projects, or sites within specific overlay areas. Security lighting overrides and safety-based deviations may be granted administratively (TBD — verify). `needs-verification`

## Calculation Examples

Illustrative only — verify all standards before use: A photometric plan for a commercial parking lot must demonstrate that average foot-candle levels across the lot meet the minimum for safety, that peak levels do not exceed the maximum, and that measured foot-candles at adjacent property lines fall within the trespass limits. Photometric plans are prepared by a lighting engineer or designer and submitted with the PDP application.

## Related Zones

Lighting standards apply across commercial, industrial, and multifamily zones citywide. Additional requirements may apply within the Chandler Airport Environs Overlay (aviation obstruction lighting considerations) or within the downtown overlay (pedestrian-scale lighting design requirements). Verify against applicable overlay sections.
