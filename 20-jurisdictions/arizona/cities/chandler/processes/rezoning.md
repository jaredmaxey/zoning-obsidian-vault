# Chandler Rezoning

## Summary

A rezoning (zone change) is the legislative act of changing a parcel's assigned zoning district designation under the Chandler Unified Development Manual. Rezonings are required when a proposed development's use or intensity cannot be accommodated within the existing zone district's permitted uses or dimensional standards. Because Chandler's zoning must be consistent with the General Plan, a rezoning that diverges from the current General Plan Future Land Use designation must be preceded by a [[Chandler General Plan Amendment]]. See also [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] for the cross-jurisdictional concept note.

## Triggering Conditions

A rezoning is required when a developer or property owner needs a different base zone to permit the intended use or development intensity. Common triggers include conversion of commercial land to mixed-use or residential, intensification of an industrial site to employment/tech uses, or infill projects requiring a higher-density designation than the current zone allows. Chandler also allows planned development rezoning (similar to a [[Planned Unit Development (PUD)]]) for larger projects that warrant customized development standards — exact UDM category and threshold TBD. `needs-verification`

## Decision-Maker

The **Planning & Zoning Commission** holds a public hearing and makes a recommendation (approval, approval with conditions, or denial). The **City Council** then holds its own public hearing and makes the final legislative decision. Both hearings are noticed publicly. ## Findings Required

For a rezoning, City Council and P&Z Commission generally assess: consistency with the General Plan, compatibility with surrounding uses and zoning, adequacy of infrastructure (traffic, utilities, drainage), and whether the proposed zoning serves the public interest and community development goals. Specific findings language TBD — verify against Chandler UDM. `needs-verification` Unlike quasi-judicial processes (variances, CUPs), the legislative standard for rezonings is broader policy judgment rather than enumerated findings.

## Appeal Path

Rezoning decisions by City Council are legislative actions. They are generally not subject to administrative appeal and are instead challengeable in Superior Court. Arizona law imposes specific timelines for legal challenge of legislative land use decisions (TBD — verify against ARS). `needs-verification`

## Common Pitfalls

- Proceeding without first confirming General Plan consistency — if a GPA is needed and not filed concurrently, the rezoning cannot be approved.
- Underestimating the political dimension: rezonings require City Council votes, meaning neighborhood opposition can be decisive.
- Inadequate conceptual site plan — even though a rezoning does not approve a specific design, a believable development concept helps decision-makers understand the project's context and impact.
- Missing noticing deadlines: Arizona law and Chandler's UDM require mailed and posted notice within specified radii and timeframes (TBD — verify). `needs-verification`
- Not requesting a pre-application conference — Chandler's Planning Division offers pre-app meetings that can identify deal-breakers early.
