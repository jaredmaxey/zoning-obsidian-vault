# Chandler Preliminary Development Plan Review

## Summary

Chandler's development review process evaluates proposed projects at the site plan level to ensure compliance with UDM development standards (site design, parking, landscaping, signage, architecture, access, drainage, and utility coordination) before building permits are issued. Chandler uses a Preliminary Development Plan (PDP) or equivalent review mechanism (exact terminology and process steps TBD — verify against current UDM). `needs-verification` This review bridges the gap between zoning entitlement (which establishes allowed uses and intensities) and building permit issuance (which verifies code compliance at the construction drawing level). See [[Site Plan Review]] for the cross-jurisdictional concept.

## Triggering Conditions

A development plan review is generally required for all new non-single-family commercial, industrial, multifamily, and mixed-use development, and for substantial additions or change of use. Minor permits and tenant improvements may be exempt from full development plan review (threshold TBD — verify against Chandler UDM). `needs-verification`

## Common Pitfalls

- Submitting an incomplete plan set — Chandler's technical staff review all discipline requirements concurrently; missing a plan sheet (e.g., photometrics or landscape) typically results in a completeness rejection and resets the review clock.
- Ignoring UDM development standards during design: parking layout, landscape coverage percentages, and building setbacks built into the architectural drawings early prevent costly redesigns during review.
- Not coordinating with Engineering and Public Works early on drainage, curb cuts, and utility service — these departments review concurrently and their comments can require significant site redesign.
- Underestimating the time to address multiple rounds of comments, especially for larger mixed-use or industrial projects.
