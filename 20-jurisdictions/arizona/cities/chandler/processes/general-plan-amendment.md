# Chandler General Plan Amendment

## Summary

A General Plan Amendment (GPA) is a legislative action that changes a parcel's or area's Future Land Use designation within Chandler's adopted General Plan. In Arizona, municipalities operate under state enabling statutes (ARS Title 9) that require zoning decisions to be consistent with the adopted General Plan; if a proposed rezoning is inconsistent with the current General Plan designation, a GPA must be approved first. GPAs are therefore the foundational upstream step for projects that require a land use category change — converting commercial land to residential, or agricultural/open space to employment, for example. See also [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] for the cross-jurisdictional concept overview.

## Triggering Conditions

A GPA is required when a property owner or developer seeks a zoning designation that is inconsistent with the parcel's current General Plan Future Land Use designation. Minor amendments may be processed administratively on a rolling basis; major or area-wide amendments may be scheduled on an annual or semi-annual cycle depending on Chandler's amendment calendar (TBD — verify against UDM procedural schedule). `needs-verification`

## Decision-Maker

The **City Council** makes the final legislative decision on all General Plan amendments, following a recommendation from the **Planning & Zoning Commission**. Both bodies hold public hearings. Because a GPA is a legislative act, the standard of review differs from quasi-judicial actions — the Council exercises broad policy discretion. ## Findings Required

For a GPA, City Council must generally find that the proposed amendment is consistent with the overall goals and policies of the General Plan and serves the public interest. Specific findings language TBD — verify against Chandler UDM or General Plan amendment procedures. `needs-verification` Because this is a legislative action, the findings standard is less prescriptive than quasi-judicial findings (e.g., variance), but the applicant should articulate how the amendment furthers growth management goals, infrastructure capacity, and community character objectives.

## Appeal Path

## Common Pitfalls

- Filing a rezoning application before securing a GPA when the proposed zoning is inconsistent with the General Plan — this results in a procedural delay or denial.
- Underestimating the policy narrative required: staff and commissioners evaluate how the amendment aligns with citywide growth strategy, not just parcel-level feasibility.
- Insufficient neighborhood outreach prior to public hearings — Chandler's process, like most Arizona cities, benefits from proactive engagement. See [[Neighborhood Outreach]] and [[Public Hearing Process]].
- Missing the annual or semi-annual amendment cycle if Chandler limits GPA filing windows.
