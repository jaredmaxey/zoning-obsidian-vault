# Chandler Subdivision Platting

## Summary

Subdivision platting in Chandler is governed by the subdivision standards within the Chandler UDM and by Arizona's enabling statutes for municipal subdivision control (ARS Title 9). The process divides parcels into two or more lots, blocks, or tracts and creates legally recorded property boundaries. The subdivision process runs alongside (and must be consistent with) applicable zoning entitlements: a parcel must be correctly zoned before a plat can receive final approval. Both residential and commercial/industrial subdivision plats proceed through a preliminary plat and final plat track. See [[Subdivision Approval]] for the cross-jurisdictional concept.

## Triggering Conditions

Subdivision platting is required whenever land is divided into two or more lots, tracts, or parcels for sale, lease, or development, or whenever public infrastructure (streets, alleys, drainage easements, utility easements) is being dedicated to the city. ## Common Pitfalls

- Proceeding with a preliminary plat before securing underlying zoning — the plat must be consistent with current zoning at the time of final plat approval.
- Incomplete improvement plans at the final plat stage — Chandler's Engineering Department reviews infrastructure plans concurrently and incomplete sets delay recording.
- Failing to execute the subdivision improvement agreement or provide adequate financial assurances for off-site improvements.
- Misunderstanding the relationship between plat approval and development plan (PDP) approval — both may be required for a project and may proceed on parallel tracks, but each has distinct submittal and approval requirements.
- Not accounting for Maricopa County recorder requirements when preparing the final plat for recording.
