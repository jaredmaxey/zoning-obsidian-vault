# Peoria, Arizona

## Overview

Peoria is a large Northwest Valley city in Maricopa County, stretching from the established South Peoria urban core northward into vast desert terrain with significant undeveloped acreage. This land area gives Peoria a longer development runway than many Valley peers and makes it a consistent target for master-planned community (MPC) entitlements, large-lot residential, and regional retail projects.

The city's economic base includes healthcare (Banner Health is a major employer), professional services, retail trade, and emerging technology/industrial uses in its newer sections. The Peoria Sports Complex (spring training for the San Diego Padres and Seattle Mariners) adds hospitality and retail demand near the stadium corridor.

For CRE practitioners, Peoria offers both infill and greenfield opportunity. Infill and mixed-use redevelopment concentrate near the 83rd Avenue and Bell Road commercial corridors, while large master-planned residential and commercial projects push northward. A [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]] or [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] is frequently the entry point for larger greenfield plays.

## Governing Body

Peoria operates under a Mayor-Council government. The Planning and Zoning Commission reviews and makes recommendations on land-use applications; the City Council makes final decisions on rezonings and general plan amendments. A Development Services Director and Zoning Administrator handle administrative functions.
