# Yuma County, Arizona

## Overview

Yuma County occupies the southwestern corner of Arizona, bordered by California to the west and Mexico to the south, with the Colorado River forming the California boundary. The county seat is Yuma. The county's economy is anchored by agriculture — the Yuma area is one of the most productive winter vegetable growing regions in North America, relying on an extensive system of irrigation canals fed by Colorado River water rights. The U.S. military (Yuma Proving Ground and Marine Corps Air Station Yuma) also plays a significant role in the local economy and land use.

The county's unincorporated areas include irrigated agricultural land (the dominant land use by acreage), the suburban fringe of the City of Yuma, the communities of San Luis (also incorporating as San Luis, AZ), Somerton, and Wellton, and rural desert land to the north and east. From a CRE perspective, [[Land]] plays in Yuma County frequently involve agricultural-to-residential or agricultural-to-industrial conversion, driven by population growth from the Yuma urban area and cross-border economic activity. Logistics and warehousing development near the I-8 corridor is a growing industrial use type.

The Yuma County Comprehensive Plan guides long-range land-use policy for unincorporated areas. Water rights and canal infrastructure are defining features of any agricultural [[Land]] evaluation in this county, as Colorado River water allocations are complex and tightly managed under state and federal compacts.

## Governing Body

The Yuma County Board of Supervisors (five members, elected by district) is the governing authority for land-use decisions in unincorporated Yuma County. The Board approves major rezonings, General Plan Amendments, and subdivision plats following recommendations from the Yuma County Planning and Zoning Commission. The Commission holds public hearings on most land-use applications.

## Planning Department

**Yuma County Development Services** (Planning and Zoning division) administers zoning, subdivision review, building permits, and inspections for unincorporated Yuma County. The department does not have authority within incorporated city or town limits. Applicants working near agricultural land or canal infrastructure should engage the department early to understand applicable overlay regulations and water-related constraints.

The ordinance addresses the full spectrum from agricultural to urban uses, with particular attention to the transition from irrigated farmland to suburban development.

## Permit Process

Development in unincorporated Yuma County generally follows this sequence (high-level; verify current process with Development Services):

1. **Pre-Application Meeting** — recommended; staff identifies water/irrigation, military compatibility, and entitlement issues
2. **Zoning / General Plan Review** — confirm current zoning and Comprehensive Plan land-use designation; verify no military compatibility zone overlay
3. **Entitlement (if required)** — [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]], [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]], or [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] through Planning and Zoning Commission and Board of Supervisors
4. **Subdivision Plat (if applicable)** — [[Subdivision Approval]] via subdivision review track; includes irrigation district coordination for agricultural land conversions
5. **Site Plan / Development Plan Review** — for commercial, industrial, and multifamily projects
6. **Building Permit** — submitted after entitlement; includes plan review
7. **Inspections and Certificate of Occupancy**
