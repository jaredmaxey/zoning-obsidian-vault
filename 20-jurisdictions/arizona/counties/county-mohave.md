# Mohave County, Arizona

## Overview

Mohave County is located in the northwestern corner of Arizona, bordered by Nevada and California along the Colorado River. The county seat is Kingman. The county's major population centers — Lake Havasu City, Bullhead City (adjacent to Laughlin, Nevada), and Kingman — include both incorporated areas and extensive unincorporated communities that depend directly on county land-use regulation. Lake Havasu City is the county's largest incorporated city; Bullhead City and Kingman are also incorporated.

The county's unincorporated areas include established residential communities (many of which developed historically as unincorporated subdivisions with minimal services), retirement and resort-oriented communities along the Colorado River, and large tracts of desert and agricultural land. From a CRE standpoint, Mohave County offers lower land costs than Phoenix metro markets, with recreational and retirement demand driving residential and hospitality-related [[Entitlement Play]] opportunities, particularly near the river corridor. Industrial and logistics development along I-40 near Kingman is a secondary CRE driver.

The Mohave County Comprehensive Plan (or General Plan) guides long-range land-use for unincorporated areas. Water availability varies significantly across the county — Colorado River water is allocated through complex interstate compacts and state contracts, while groundwater serves inland communities — making water supply verification an important step in [[Land]] due diligence.

## Governing Body

The Mohave County Board of Supervisors (five members, elected by district) is the governing authority for land-use decisions in unincorporated Mohave County. The Board approves major rezonings, General Plan Amendments, and subdivision plats following recommendations from the Mohave County Planning and Zoning Commission. The Commission holds public hearings on most land-use applications.

## Planning Department

**Mohave County Planning and Zoning Department** administers zoning, subdivision review, building permits, and inspections for unincorporated Mohave County. The department does not have authority within incorporated city or town limits. Given the large geographic spread of the county, applicants should confirm which office location serves their parcel.

## Subdivision Regulations

Mohave County administers subdivision of unincorporated land under its **Subdivision Regulations** (exact title — TBD — verify), consistent with Arizona Revised Statutes Title 11. The county has a history of large historic subdivisions (some platted decades ago with minimal infrastructure), and infill development or re-subdivision of these older plats may involve additional complexity. Process elements include:

- **Preliminary Plat** review for layout, access, infrastructure, and water/sewer adequacy
- **Final Plat** recordation after conditions and improvement agreements or bonds are in place
- **[[Subdivision Approval]]** by the Board of Supervisors for major subdivisions; administrative tracks for minor subdivisions (thresholds TBD — verify)

## Permit Process

Development in unincorporated Mohave County generally follows this sequence (high-level; verify current process with Planning and Zoning):

1. **Pre-Application Meeting** — recommended; staff identifies applicable standards, water availability issues, and entitlement path
2. **Zoning / General Plan Review** — confirm current zoning and General Plan designation
3. **Entitlement (if required)** — [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]], [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]], or [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] through Planning and Zoning Commission and Board of Supervisors
4. **Subdivision Plat (if applicable)** — [[Subdivision Approval]] via subdivision review track
5. **Site Plan / Development Plan Review** — for commercial, industrial, and multifamily projects
6. **Building Permit** — submitted after entitlement; includes plan review
7. **Inspections and Certificate of Occupancy**
