# Yavapai County, Arizona

## Overview

Yavapai County is a large, geographically diverse county in central Arizona, anchored by the Prescott metropolitan area. The county seat is Prescott. Yavapai is Arizona's second-largest county by land area, encompassing terrain from the high-desert plateau and ponderosa pine forests around Prescott to the lower Verde River corridor and red rock country near Sedona (which straddles the Yavapai/Coconino county line). The county's unincorporated areas are extensive and include rural residential communities, ranches, agricultural land, resort and vacation properties, and communities that are largely dependent on county services and county land-use regulation.

The Prescott metro's appeal as a retirement and second-home destination, combined with its relative affordability compared to Phoenix, has driven steady population growth in both the incorporated cities and in unincorporated Yavapai County. This creates a consistent pipeline of residential [[Land]] entitlement and [[Entitlement Play]] opportunities in the county's unincorporated areas. The county's Community Development Department administers land-use regulations under the Yavapai County Development Code.

Yavapai County's General Plan addresses long-range land-use, transportation, housing, and environmental goals for unincorporated areas. Water availability — particularly groundwater in rural areas outside municipal water service — is a relevant constraint for rural development and must be addressed in some subdivision proceedings.

## Governing Body

The Yavapai County Board of Supervisors (five members, elected by district) is the governing authority for land-use decisions in unincorporated Yavapai County. The Board approves major rezonings, General Plan Amendments, and subdivision plats following recommendations from the Yavapai County Planning and Zoning Commission. The Commission holds public hearings on most land-use applications.

## Planning Department

**Yavapai County Community Development Department** administers zoning, subdivision review, building permits, and inspections for unincorporated Yavapai County. The department does not have authority within incorporated city or town limits. It provides pre-application assistance and staff-level guidance for prospective applicants.

## Subdivision Regulations

Yavapai County administers subdivision of unincorporated land under the subdivision provisions of the Yavapai County Development Code, consistent with Arizona Revised Statutes Title 11. Process elements include:

- **Preliminary Plat** review for layout, access, infrastructure, and water adequacy
- **Final Plat** recordation after conditions and improvement assurances are in place
- **[[Subdivision Approval]]** by the Board of Supervisors for major subdivisions; administrative tracks for minor subdivisions (thresholds TBD — verify)
- For rural subdivisions outside municipal water service, proof of adequate water supply is required
