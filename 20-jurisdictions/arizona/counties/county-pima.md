# Pima County, Arizona

## Overview

Pima County is located in southern Arizona and anchors the Tucson metropolitan area, the state's second-largest metro. The county seat is Tucson, though the City of Tucson and other incorporated municipalities exercise their own zoning authority within their boundaries. Pima County's Development Services Department regulates the substantial unincorporated areas that surround these cities — encompassing rural desert land, master-planned communities on the urban fringe, agricultural land in the Santa Cruz Valley, and tribal land adjacencies.

The county's development pattern reflects southern Arizona's distinctive geography: mountain preserves (including Saguaro National Park on both the east and west sides of Tucson), riparian corridors, and a desert environment that shapes both land suitability and regulatory standards. The county has historically emphasized environmental and hillside sensitivity in its zoning and development standards — an important consideration for [[Land]] evaluation and [[Entitlement Play]] feasibility in unincorporated areas.

The Pima County Comprehensive Plan (formerly "Pima County Comprehensive Land Use Plan") guides long-range land-use policy for unincorporated areas. The county also coordinates with the regional planning organization on transportation and growth management.

## Governing Body

The Pima County Board of Supervisors (five members, elected by district) is the ultimate decision-making authority for land-use matters in unincorporated Pima County. Major land-use applications — rezonings, General Plan Amendments, and policy decisions — require Board approval following a recommendation from the Pima County Planning and Zoning Commission. The Commission serves as the primary public hearing body for most entitlement applications.

## Planning Department

**Pima County Development Services Department** handles zoning administration, subdivision review, building permits, and inspections for unincorporated Pima County. The department does not have authority within incorporated city or town limits. The department administers the Pima County Zoning Code and processes entitlement applications.

- Primary website: https://www.pima.gov/1426/Zoning-Code
- Development Services also manages code enforcement and floodplain administration for unincorporated areas.

## Subdivision Regulations

- **Preliminary Plat** review for layout, access, infrastructure, and consistency with the zoning code and Comprehensive Plan
- **Final Plat** recordation after conditions are satisfied and improvements are constructed or financially guaranteed
- **Grading and drainage review** administered through Development Services engineering

## Permit Process

Development in unincorporated Pima County generally follows this sequence (high-level; verify current process and timelines with Development Services):

1. **Pre-Application Meeting** — recommended prior to formal submittal to identify applicable standards and entitlement path
2. **Zoning/Plan Check** — confirm parcel's zoning designation and Comprehensive Plan land-use designation
3. **Entitlement (if required)** — [[Zone Change (Rezoning)]], [[Conditional Use Permit (CUP)]], or [[General Plan Amendment (GPA)]] through the Planning and Zoning Commission and Board of Supervisors
4. **Subdivision Plat (if applicable)** — [[Subdivision Approval]] via the subdivision review track
5. **Site Plan / Development Plan Review** — staff or commission level depending on application type
6. **Building Permit** — submitted to Development Services after entitlement clearance; includes plan review
7. **Inspections and Certificate of Occupancy** — coordinated with county building inspection
